## financials
Marcus presented Q4 numbers. Revenue came in at $4.2M which is actually 12% above forecast ($3.75M was the target). But costs were also up — $3.8M total opex vs $3.2M budgeted. The overage was mainly from the unplanned hiring push in engineering (8 new hires in oct/nov) plus the AWS bill which ballooned to like $340K/mo (was $210K in Q3). Marcus flagged that if we dont get the infra costs under control we'll burn through the remaining runway ($6.1M in bank as of dec 1) in about 14 months instead of the planned 19 months.

Sarah pushed back saying the eng hires were necessary for the enterprise product launch. Dave agreed but said we need to see ROI by Q2 2026 or consider cuts.

Net burn was $890K for the month of november. Gross margin dropped from 71% to 64%.

## product update
Lisa showed the enterprise product demo. Launch date moved AGAIN — now targeting feb 15 2026 (was jan 15, before that was dec 1). Main blockers: SOC2 audit taking longer than expected (auditor found 3 critical gaps in access controls), and the SSO integration with okta is broken after their api change in november.

Key metrics from the beta: 12 enterprise clients in pilot, average deal size $45K ARR, but only 3 have signed LOIs. Lisa mentioned churn on the SMB side ticked up to 4.8% monthly (was 3.1% in Q3). Dave asked why and Lisa said its mainly competitors offering free tiers that undercut us on the low end.

## hiring & team
We're now at 67 people total. Engineering is 34. Sales team grew to 12 (was 8 in Q3). Sarah wants to hire a VP Sales — budget approved up to $280K base + equity. Marcus cautioned about adding more headcount before enterprise revenue materializes. 

Ana (who joined late, missed the financials) asked about the DEI metrics. HR report says we're at 38% women overall, 22% in engineering. Ana wants quarterly reporting on this going forward.

## legal stuff (before James left)
James briefed on the patent situation. We filed 3 new patents in Q4 (total portfolio now 11). One existing patent (#US20230145) is being challenged by CompetitorX — hearing scheduled for March 2026. James estimates 60-70% chance we prevail but said worst case we'd need to redesign the recommendation engine which could take 4-6 months.

Also the EU data processing agreement needs to be updated for the new clients in Germany and France. Deadline is jan 31 2026 or we risk GDPR fines. James said its "mostly done, just needs CEO signature."

## strategic discussion
Big debate about pricing strategy. Current pricing:
- Starter: $29/mo (being undercut by competitors)

Dave proposed killing the Starter tier entirely and repositioning as enterprise-only. Sarah disagreed — says SMB is still 60% of revenue ($2.5M of the $4.2M). Compromise: raise Starter to $49/mo and add usage limits. Vote was 3-2 in favor (Dave and Ana voted against, wanted enterprise-only).
