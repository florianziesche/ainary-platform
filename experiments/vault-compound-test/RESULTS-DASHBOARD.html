Key Findings

1. The Big Jump Is From 0 Links to 3 Links
Flat‚ÜíZK quality: +33 points (Cohen's d = 1.84, very large). ZK‚ÜíGraph-First: +5 points (d = 0.43, small). Adding links at all matters enormously. Adding MORE links has diminishing returns.
2. PARA ‚â§ Flat ‚Äî Folders Don't Help Knowledge Compounding
PARA scored lower than Flat on 3/10 questions. Actionability-based taxonomy adds navigation friction without creating inference paths. For knowledge work, PARA is a filing system, not a thinking system.
3. MOCs Are Indexes, Not Intelligence
MOC-Hybrid scored best on decision retrieval (Q3 = 9) but poorly on emergence (avg 5.3). Hub-and-spoke topology requires 2 hops (note‚ÜíMOC‚Üínote) vs. ZK's 1 hop (note‚Üínote). MOCs organize; links compound.
4. Metadata Enables Structural Gap Analysis
Graph-First's rich frontmatter (type, confidence, source) allowed identifying blind spots by querying what's MISSING ‚Äî zero case studies, zero user research, zero competitive analysis. Zettelkasten finds gaps through absent links; metadata finds gaps through absent categories.
5. Practical Winner ‚â† Theoretical Winner
Graph-First wins on paper (92.1 vs 83.4). But 5+ links per note is unsustainable long-term. Zettelkasten's 3-link minimum is achievable. A system that compounds at 56% emergence reliably beats one that compounds at 64% theoretically but degrades under maintenance pressure.

Methodology

Content: 50 identical notes extracted from 9 Ainary Research Reports (AR-001‚ÄìAR-009): 30 atomic claims, 10 key concepts, 5 cross-cutting insights, 5 decisions.

Vaults: Same content organized per 5 architectures: Flat (no links, no folders), PARA (4 folders by actionability), Zettelkasten (atomic notes, 3+ links, sequence IDs), MOC-Hybrid (numbered folders, MOC index files, moderate links), Graph-First (5+ links, rich frontmatter, Dataview queries).

Test: 10 questions in 3 categories (Retrieval, Inference, Emergence). Each vault scored independently on 5 metrics: Answer Quality (0-10), Time to Answer (seconds), Sources Referenced (count), Emergence Score (0-10), Self-Reference (yes/no).

Limitations: Single AI rater. Static test (no temporal compounding). Small sample (10 questions). AI reading patterns ‚â† human reading patterns. Maintenance burden not measured.
