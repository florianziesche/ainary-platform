# [[VC]] Terminology

*Reference for conversations, interviews, and your own fund.*

---

## Fund Structure

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **GP (General Partner)** | Fund managers who make investment decisions and manage the fund |
| **LP (Limited Partner)** | Investors who provide capital but don't manage the fund |
| **Management Fee** | Annual fee (typically 2%) charged by GP to cover operations |
| **Carried Interest (Carry)** | GP's share of profits (typically 20%) above a hurdle rate |
| **Hurdle Rate** | Minimum return LPs must receive before GP gets carry |
| **Fund Size / AUM** | Total capital committed to the fund |
| **Vintage Year** | Year the fund started making investments |
| **Fund Life** | Typical 10 years: ~5 investment period + ~5 harvest period |

---

## Investment Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Pre-money Valuation** | Company value before new investment |
| **Post-money Valuation** | Company value after new investment (pre + new capital) |
| **Pro Rata** | Right to maintain ownership % in future rounds |
| **Anti-dilution** | Protection against down rounds |
| **Liquidation Preference** | Order and multiple of payout in exit (1x, 2x, participating) |
| **SAFE** | Simple Agreement for Future Equity (convertible instrument) |
| **Convertible Note** | Debt that converts to equity at next round |
| **Cap Table** | Spreadsheet showing ownership percentages |

---

## Deal Flow & Process

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Deal Flow** | Pipeline of investment opportunities |
| **Sourcing** | Finding new deals (inbound, outbound, referrals) |
| **Screening** | Initial evaluation of opportunities |
| **Due Diligence** | Deep research before investing |
| **Term Sheet** | Non-binding offer outlining deal terms |
| **IC (Investment Committee)** | Internal decision-making body |
| **Partner Meeting** | Founder presents to full partnership |
| **Conviction** | Strong belief in a deal (required to invest) |

---

## Stages

| Stage | Typical Check | What's Proven |
|-------|---------------|---------------|
| **Pre-seed** | $100K-$500K | Idea, maybe prototype |
| **Seed** | $500K-$3M | Product, early users |
| **Series A** | $5M-$15M | PMF, repeatable growth |
| **Series B** | $15M-$50M | Scaling, unit economics |
| **Growth** | $50M+ | Market leader, path to IPO |

---

## Metrics VCs Care About

| Metric | What It Measures |
|--------|------------------|
| **ARR/MRR** | Annual/Monthly Recurring Revenue |
| **Growth Rate** | MoM or YoY revenue growth |
| **Burn Rate** | Monthly cash spend |
| **Runway** | Months of cash remaining |
| **CAC** | Customer Acquisition Cost |
| **LTV** | Lifetime Value of a customer |
| **LTV:CAC** | Ratio (3:1+ is healthy) |
| **Net Retention** | Revenue kept + expanded from existing customers |
| **Churn** | % of customers/revenue lost |
| **Gross Margin** | Revenue minus COGS |

---

## Returns & Performance

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **TVPI** | Total Value to Paid-In (realized + unrealized / invested) |
| **DPI** | Distributed to Paid-In (cash returned / invested) |
| **IRR** | Internal Rate of Return (time-weighted) |
| **MOIC** | Multiple on Invested Capital |
| **Power Law** | Most returns come from 1-2 outliers |
| **J-Curve** | Fund value dips early (fees, markdowns) before rising |

## NYC [[VC]] Slang

- **"We're not leading"** — We'll follow if someone else leads
- **"Keep us updated"** — Polite no (unless they follow up)
- **"We're at capacity"** — Fund is fully deployed
- **"Let me check with partners"** — Maybe, maybe not
- **"Strong pass"** — Definite no
