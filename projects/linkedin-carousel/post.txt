I built an AI that predicts local elections without polls.
On March 8, 2026, we will know if it works.

Most German towns under 100K population have zero polling data. Campaigns run on gut feeling. Local elections are a black box. So I built a system that analyzes public data, cross-city patterns, and structural factors to forecast outcomes. The test case: Friedberg, Bavaria. 6 candidates. March 8, 2026.

Here is what makes it different: we do not pretend to know. Every claim is labeled E (evidenced fact), I (interpretation), J (judgment call), or A (assumption). Our ranges are wide because uncertainty is real. This is not predictive magic. It is structured intelligence.

The insight: incumbents lose when fragmented. In 5 of 8 analyzed cities, sitting mayors dropped when facing 3+ challengers. Friedberg fits the pattern. Roland Eichmann (SPD) won 2020 with 52%. This time, he lost his coalition partner and faces five opponents. Our model says 38-48% in round one, with runoff probability at 85%. Stephanie Böhm (Grüne) projects 22-30%. Christian Stamp (Parteifreie) at 10-16%.

The stack: 300+ public sources per city, NLP extraction, no surveys. We analyze what is already there but never aggregated. Social posts, council minutes, historical results, coalition shifts, demographic trends. The model finds patterns that gut feeling misses.

March 8 is judgment day. Every forecast gets graded publicly. If we are wrong, we will show how and why. If we are close, we scale to 2,000+ German towns with the same data desert problem. Think Nate Silver for local politics, but starting with transparency over confidence.
