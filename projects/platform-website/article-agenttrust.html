State of AI Agent Trust 2026 — Why We Published Our Research Open Source

Florian Ziesche · Feb 16, 2026 · 5 min read

We built a multi-agent AI system to produce research reports. Then we pointed it at itself — at the question of whether enterprises actually trust AI agents to do real work.

The answer was uncomfortable. So we published everything.

What the system found

The report — AR-001, "State of AI Agent Trust 2026" — synthesized 24 independent sources, from HBR surveys to academic benchmarks. Five AI agents worked in parallel: researching, analyzing, cross-verifying, challenging, and synthesizing. The full report took under 10 minutes to generate.

AI agent capability doubles every seven months. Enterprise governance updates annually. That gap isn't closing — it's accelerating. We call it the Trust Race.

Why we published it open source

If trust is the problem, you can't solve it behind closed doors.

Every claim in the report carries a confidence label — Evidenced, Interpretation, Judgment, or Assumption. The overall report confidence is 73%. Not because the research is weak, but because our central framework (the Trust Race Model) is original and hasn't been externally validated yet. We flag that openly.

A system that asks you to trust its output should be willing to show how it got there.

The full methodology, all sources, and the confidence framework are in the report. The code and data pipeline are on GitHub. Anyone can verify, critique, or build on it.

How the system works

AR-001 wasn't written by one model answering one prompt. It was produced by a pipeline of specialized agents:

  • Researcher — scans and extracts from primary sources
  • Analyst — structures findings into frameworks
  • Strategist — generates recommendations
  • Critic — cross-verifies claims, flags contradictions
  • Synthesizer — compiles the final report with confidence labels

Three synthesis rounds. Cross-verification against source material. Confidence scoring on every claim. The output is a 47-page report that would take a research team days to produce manually.

What this means for enterprises

The trust problem isn't going away by waiting. Companies that build trust infrastructure now — confidence frameworks, verification pipelines, transparent reporting — will have a structural advantage over those that don't.

That's what we're building at Ainary. Not another chatbot. A system that does the research, shows its work, and tells you where it's uncertain.
