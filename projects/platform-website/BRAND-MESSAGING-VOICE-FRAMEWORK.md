## II. Voice Attributes & Positioning on Spectrums

### Voice Spectrum Analysis

#### **Corporate ‚Üî Casual**
**Position: 65% Casual, 35% Corporate**

- **Casual elements:** First-person narrative ("I built," "I failed"), contractions, occasional humor
- **Corporate elements:** Structured deliverables, professional tone in client work, no slang
- **Why:** Casual builds relatability and authenticity; corporate signals competence

**Do:**
- "I spent 6 days building. That was dumb."
- "Here's the exact architecture that works."

**Don't:**
- "Hey fam, let's talk AI! üî•" (too casual)
- "Pursuant to our discussion regarding..." (too corporate)

---

#### **Technical ‚Üî Accessible**
**Position: 70% Technical, 30% Accessible**

- **Technical depth:** Show the architecture (RAG pipelines, multi-agent loops, API design)
- **Accessible framing:** Explain *why* it matters in business terms first, tech second
- **Why:** Technical credibility attracts serious buyers; accessibility expands reach

**Do:**
- "The RAG system uses hybrid retrieval: BM25 for exact matches, dense vectors for semantic similarity. This matters because..."
- "Hallucination rate <0.2% means 1 error per 500 queries. In legal contexts, that's the difference between usable and lawsuit-risk."

**Don't:**
- Use jargon without explanation ("We implemented a sparse-dense fusion retriever with cross-encoder reranking" ‚Äî unless audience is ML engineers)
- Dumb it down to patronizing levels ("AI is like a really smart robot!")

---

#### **Bold ‚Üî Humble**
**Position: 55% Bold, 45% Humble**

- **Bold claims:** "I replaced an entire back office with one AI agent" / "The only CNC system built by someone who's been on a shop floor"
- **Humble admissions:** "I lost ‚Ç¨2,100 to procrastination" / "This system fails when..."
- **Why:** Boldness gets attention; humility builds trust

**Do:**
- "This is the best RAG architecture for legal AI I've seen ‚Äî and here are the 3 places it still breaks."
- "I raised ‚Ç¨5.5M, but hiring was still the hardest part. Here's what I got wrong."

**Don't:**
- Arrogance without proof ("My AI is better than everyone's")
- False humility ("I'm just a simple founder..." while listing massive achievements)

---

### Voice Summary: "The Technical Operator Who Shows the Work"

**If Ainary were a person at a conference:**
- They'd skip the inspirational keynote (too vague)
- They'd attend the technical workshop (real architecture)
- They'd buy you coffee after and honestly answer "what didn't work?"
- They'd sketch the system on a napkin while explaining trade-offs
- You'd leave thinking: "This person has *done* the thing, not just talked about it"

## III. Tone Variation by Context

### **Website (Homepage, Services, Portfolio)**
**Tone:** Confident, specific, monochrome-restrained

**Attributes:**
- Short sentences. Active voice. No fluff.
- Gold accent on ONE key message per section
- Lead with outcome, follow with approach
- Transparency about when NOT to hire us

**Example copy:**
> **Hero:** "AI systems that go into production, not PowerPoints."  
> **Services:** "Legal AI that lawyers trust. <0.2% hallucination. Full citation tracking."  
> **Portfolio:** "DepoDigest: 40-hour depositions ‚Üí 4-minute executive summaries."  
> **Not a Fit:** "Don't hire us if you need enterprise sales theater. Do hire us if you need systems that ship."

---

### **Blog / Substack (Thought Leadership, Technical Deep Dives)**
**Tone:** Conversational, first-person, vulnerable + technical

**Attributes:**
- Stories first, systems second
- "Here's what I built, here's what broke, here's what I learned"
- Generous with specifics (actual prompts, architectures, numbers)
- Kintsugi philosophy: show the golden scars

**Example openings:**
> "I ran 84 AI agents in one session and didn't send a single email. That cost me ‚Ç¨2,100. Here's the system I built to never let that happen again."
>
> "Everyone building legal AI uses GPT-4 and hopes for the best. Here's the RAG architecture that actually works ‚Äî and the 3 places it still fails."

---

### **Twitter / LinkedIn (Social, Thought Leadership)**
**Tone:** Punchy, opinionated, proof-driven

**Attributes:**
- Lead with contrarian take or specific number
- One idea per post (no multi-threaded philosophical essays)
- Proof in replies (link to blog, screenshot architecture, share numbers)
- Engage, don't broadcast

**Example posts:**
> **Twitter:** "Most 'AI consultants' have never deployed a model to production. They're selling strategy decks, not systems. If you can't show me your GitHub or a live product, we're not talking about the same thing."
>
> **LinkedIn:** "I replaced my entire back office with AI agents. Cost: ‚Ç¨0/month. Output: 16 deliverables in 5 days. The bottleneck in AI isn't capability ‚Äî it's your operating system."

---

### **Consulting / Pitch Decks (Client-Facing)**
**Tone:** Professional, ROI-focused, trust-building

**Attributes:**
- Problem ‚Üí Approach ‚Üí Timeline ‚Üí Investment (clean structure)
- Proof via case studies, not credentials
- Transparency about trade-offs and limitations
- "When NOT to hire us" section builds trust

**Example structure:**
> **Problem:** "Your legal team spends 40 hours per deposition on manual review. That's ‚Ç¨8K in labor cost per case."  
> **Approach:** "We build a RAG-based analysis system that compresses 40 hours to 4 minutes. <0.2% hallucination rate. Full citations."  
> **Timeline:** "Pilot in 6 weeks. 3 depositions analyzed. You decide if ROI justifies full deployment."  
> **Investment:** "‚Ç¨2,500 for pilot. ‚Ç¨750/month after if you proceed."

---

### **Internal / Documentation (System Design, SOPs)**
**Tone:** Precise, structured, no-BS

**Attributes:**
- Markdown formatting, modular structure
- Assumptions stated upfront
- "What works / What doesn't" sections
- Designed for future-you (or another human taking over)

**Example:**
> **System: CNC Job Planner**  
> **Purpose:** Estimate production time + cost for CNC jobs from CAD files  
> **Inputs:** CAD PDF or Heidenhain CAM file  
> **Outputs:** Time per operation, material cost, total job cost  
> **Known limitations:** Can't auto-detect custom tooling; requires manual override for non-standard materials  
> **Next iteration:** Add tool library + material database

## IV. Language Guidelines (Do / Don't Examples)

### ‚úÖ **Do: Be Specific**
- "RAG system with hybrid retrieval (BM25 + dense vectors) and cross-encoder reranking"
- "Reduced hallucination rate from 12% to 0.18%"
- "‚Ç¨2,500 pilot, ‚Ç¨750/month for full deployment"

### ‚ùå **Don't: Be Vague**
- "Advanced AI solutions"
- "Cutting-edge technology"
- "Transformative results"

---

### ‚úÖ **Do: Show the Work**
- "Here's the prompt template I use for multi-agent validation"
- "I tested 3 retrieval methods. BM25 alone had 8% recall. Dense vectors had 11% recall. Hybrid had 19%."
- "I lost ‚Ç¨2,100 to procrastination. Here's the system that fixed it."

### ‚ùå **Don't: Hide the Process**
- "Our proprietary methodology delivers results"
- "We have a proven track record"
- "Trust us, it works"

---

### ‚úÖ **Do: Name the Failure**
- "This system breaks when queries are ambiguous. We're working on that."
- "I spent 6 days building instead of shipping. That was dumb."
- "The first version of CNC Planner had terrible UX. Three shop managers told me. I fixed it."

### ‚ùå **Don't: Pretend Perfection**
- "Our system handles all edge cases"
- "Zero downtime, guaranteed"
- "We never make mistakes"

---

### ‚úÖ **Do: Use Active Voice**
- "I built a system that..."
- "We reduce costs by..."
- "Florian ships AI systems to production"

### ‚ùå **Don't: Use Passive Voice**
- "A system was built that..."
- "Costs are reduced by..."
- "AI systems are shipped by our team"

---

### ‚úÖ **Do: Simplify Complexity**
- "A RAG system retrieves relevant documents, then uses an LLM to synthesize them into an answer. The trick is making retrieval accurate enough that the LLM doesn't hallucinate."

### ‚ùå **Don't: Jargon-Dump Without Context**
- "We leverage transformer-based dense embeddings with approximate nearest neighbor search via HNSW indexing to optimize semantic retrieval latency."

---

### ‚úÖ **Do: Use Numbers**
- "<0.2% hallucination rate"
- "40 hours ‚Üí 4 minutes"
- "‚Ç¨8K/month in labor costs ‚Üí ‚Ç¨750/month"

### ‚ùå **Don't: Use Generic Claims**
- "Significantly faster"
- "Much more accurate"
- "Cost-effective solution"
