Topics to Watch — February 2026

The research directions that will shape Q2. Updated monthly.

Rising
01 Agentic RAG

Shift from static retrieval to multi-step agentic retrieval. The system reasons about what to search, not just what to return.

02 Structured Memory

Graph-based and Zettelkasten-style memory outperforming flat retrieval. Context architecture matters more than context length.

03 Single vs Multi-Agent

Efficiency trade-offs becoming clearer. Multi-agent shines at complex tasks but hits cognitive limits at coordination scale.

04 Agent Safety & Governance

Growing focus on constraints, audit trails, and governance frameworks. The "how do we control this" conversation is catching up.

05 Scientific Discovery

LLMs as autonomous researchers — hypothesis generation, experiment design, literature synthesis. The most ambitious application of agentic AI.

Declining
Traditional Fine-Tuning

Replaced by in-context learning and reinforcement learning. The economics no longer justify custom model training for most use cases.

Simple Prompt Engineering

Replaced by agentic orchestration. The value moved from "how you ask" to "how you coordinate multiple systems that ask, verify, and act."

So What

The market is moving from "AI does one thing well" to "AI systems coordinate, remember, and reason." If your strategy is built on RAG pipelines or prompt libraries — it has a shelf life of 6 months. Invest in orchestration and memory architecture now.
