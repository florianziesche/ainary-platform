And they outperformed my hand-written ones. Here's exactly how.

The old way: Research a prospect for 15 minutes. Write a personalized email for 10 minutes. Send. Repeat. At best, I'd do 8 emails in a focused morning. Response rate: about 12%.

The new way:

Step 1: I built a research agent that pulls a prospect's LinkedIn activity, company news, recent funding rounds, and tech stack. Takes 45 seconds per prospect.

Step 2: The agent feeds this research into a writing system with 6 email templates I've tested over 200+ sends. It picks the template that fits the prospect's situation and personalizes every line.

Step 3: I review each email in about 90 seconds. I'm checking tone, accuracy, and whether it sounds like something I'd actually say. I edit maybe 30% of them. The rest go out as-is.

Results after 3 months:
- 20 emails in 30 minutes (vs. 8 in 3 hours)
- Response rate: 18% (up from 12%)
- Booked meetings: 2-3x more per week
- Time saved: roughly 10 hours per week on outreach alone

Why the AI emails perform better: consistency. My hand-written emails varied wildly based on my energy level, mood, and how much coffee I'd had. The AI delivers the same quality at email #20 as email #1.

The key insight: AI didn't replace the strategy. I still decide who to email, what angle to take, and when to follow up. AI just eliminated the mechanical work of executing at volume.
