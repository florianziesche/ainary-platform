## Option A: "The Agent Team" (Behind-the-Scenes)

I don't manage employees. I manage AI agents.

My company runs on 10 specialized agents:
→ One finds leads and researches prospects
→ One writes and personalizes outreach
→ One handles code and deployments
→ One manages content across platforms
→ One reviews everything before it ships

Each has a role, context, and quality gates. Like a team, except they cost $15/night instead of $15,000/month.

Last week they produced 50 deliverables overnight. Research reports, sales emails, application packages.

The catch? I still have to press send.

AI doesn't replace the hardest part of building a company: putting your work in front of other people.

The real skill in 2026 isn't prompting. It's orchestrating systems and having the courage to ship.

More on how this works in my newsletter — link in comments.

## Option B: "The Honest Numbers" (Data + Vulnerability)

Here are my actual numbers from last week:

Outreach emails prepared: 20. Sent: 0.
VC applications drafted: 10. Submitted: 0.
Content pieces created: 15. Published: 2.

Prepared-to-sent ratio: 55:2.

I built an AI system that produces more in one night than I used to in a week. And still, the bottleneck is me.

AI is a mirror. It shows you exactly where your real problems are. And they're never technical.

The founder who masters AI orchestration has an edge. But the founder who presses send has a business.

Working on closing this gap. Will report back.

## Option C: "The Knowledge Gap" (CNC/Industry Angle)

In German manufacturing, 13-20 million workers will retire by 2036.

Most of their knowledge isn't written down. It lives in their heads. When they leave, it leaves with them.

I built a tool that captures manufacturing knowledge before it walks out the door. CNC work preparation that used to take 90 minutes now takes 10.

Not because AI is smarter than the experienced Meister. Because it never forgets, never retires, and works at 2 AM.

The companies that digitize their manufacturing knowledge now will survive the skills gap. The ones that don't will lose customers to those who did.

This isn't an AI hype story. It's a demographic reality.
