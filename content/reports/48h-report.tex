\section{How It Works}

The human provides direction, makes strategic decisions, and reviews output. The AI:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Researches} --- web search, source synthesis, competitive analysis
  \item \textbf{Writes} --- articles, emails, pitches, cover letters in calibrated voice
  \item \textbf{Builds} --- websites, PDFs, dashboards, documentation
  \item \textbf{Challenges itself} --- deploys adversarial ``red team'' agents to find weaknesses in its own work
  \item \textbf{Organizes} --- syncs files, maintains memory, prepares briefings
  \item \textbf{Works in parallel} --- multiple specialized agents tackle different aspects simultaneously
\end{itemize}

\begin{highlightbox}
\textbf{The key insight:} The AI doesn't replace the human's judgment. It amplifies their capacity. Strategy stays human. Execution scales through AI.
\end{highlightbox}

\section{The Process That Emerged}

After 48 hours of iteration, a validated workflow crystallized:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em, itemsep=6pt]
  \item \textbf{Fast first draft} --- capture the initial direction quickly
  \item \textbf{Multi-perspective research} --- 3 parallel agents with different lenses (strategy, positioning, adversarial)
  \item \textbf{Human synthesis} --- the primary agent integrates all perspectives (sub-agents produce raw material, the orchestrator connects dots)
  \item \textbf{Explicit comparison} --- v1 vs v2 table makes improvement visible and traceable
\end{enumerate}

\vspace{12pt}

This isn't automation. It's a new way of working.
