# Step 4: VC Terminology Glossary

## Fund Structure Terms

**General Partner (GP):** The individual(s) who manage a venture fund. GPs make investment decisions, source deals, and support portfolio companies. They typically invest 1â€“2% of fund capital ("GP commit") and earn management fees + carried interest.

**Limited Partner (LP):** Investors who provide capital to a venture fund but don't participate in day-to-day management. LPs include pension funds, endowments, family offices, fund-of-funds, and HNW individuals.

**Management Fee:** Annual fee paid to GPs for fund operations, typically 2% of committed capital during investment period (4â€“5 years), then 2% of invested capital during harvest period. Covers salaries, office, travel, and operational costs.

**Carried Interest (Carry):** GP's share of fund profits, typically 20% after returning LP capital + preferred return. This is the primary economic incentive for GPs. Standard structure: 80/20 split (LP/GP) above hurdle.

**Preferred Return (Hurdle Rate):** Minimum annual return LPs must receive before GPs earn carry, typically 8%. Protects LP downside and aligns GP incentives with strong performance.

**GP Commit:** Capital the GP personally invests in the fund, typically 1â€“2% of fund size. Demonstrates skin in the game to LPs.

---

## Investment Terms

**Pre-Money Valuation:** Company valuation before new investment. If pre-money is $10M and investment is $2M â†’ post-money is $12M â†’ investor owns 16.7%.

**Post-Money Valuation:** Company valuation after investment. Post-money = Pre-money + Investment Amount.

**Pro-Rata Rights:** Right to invest in future rounds to maintain ownership percentage. Critical for seed investors to avoid dilution in Series A+.

**Liquidation Preference:** Determines payout order in exit/liquidation. "1x non-participating" = investors get money back first, then remaining proceeds to common. "1x participating" = get money back AND share in remaining.

**Anti-Dilution Protection:** Protects investors if company raises at lower valuation (down round). Weighted average anti-dilution is standard; full ratchet is aggressive.

**SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity):** Y Combinator-created instrument for seed investing. Converts to equity at next priced round. Simpler and cheaper than convertible notes.

**Convertible Note:** Debt instrument that converts to equity, with interest rate and maturity date. More complex than SAFEs but provides debt holder protections.

---

## Deal Flow & Process Terms

**Deal Flow:** Pipeline of investment opportunities. Quality > quantity. The best VCs convert 1â€“2% of deals they see.

**Due Diligence:** Investigation before investment â€” team, market, product/tech, financials, legal, customer references, competitive landscape. Typically 2â€“6 weeks for seed.

**Term Sheet:** Non-binding document outlining key investment terms: valuation, amount, board seats, protective provisions. Precedes definitive legal documents.

**Cap Table:** Ownership spreadsheet showing who owns what % â€” founders, employees (options), and investors across all rounds.

**Information Rights:** Right to receive periodic financial reports from portfolio company. Standard for investors above a minimum threshold.

---

## Return Metrics

**TVPI (Total Value to Paid-In):** Total fund value (realized + unrealized) / capital called. TVPI 3.0x = fund worth 3x what LPs invested.

**DPI (Distributions to Paid-In):** Cash returned to LPs / capital called. Shows actual realized returns (money in LP pockets).

**IRR (Internal Rate of Return):** Annualized return accounting for timing of cash flows. Top-quartile seed funds target 25%+ net IRR.

**MOIC (Multiple on Invested Capital):** Simple return multiple. Total value / invested capital. Doesn't account for time.

**Power Law:** In venture, a small number of investments generate the vast majority of returns. One 50x return matters more than ten 2x returns.
