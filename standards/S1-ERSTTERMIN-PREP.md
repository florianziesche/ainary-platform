## 4. PROCESS

### Step 1: Research Counterpart (10 min)

**Person Research:**
- LinkedIn profile (role, tenure, background, recent posts)

### Step 2: Craft the Offer (5 min)

**Rule: No generic "AI consulting" pitch.** Tailor to their world.

### Step 3: Anticipate Objections (5 min)

- Acknowledge the concern (never dismiss)

### Step 4: Prepare the Ask (3 min)

- **Cold/First contact:** Ask for a follow-up meeting with decision-makers

- **Hot/They reached out:** Ask for timeline and budget confirmation

## 7. KNOWN FAILURE MODES

| Failure | How it manifests | Prevention |
|---------|-----------------|------------|
| **Monolog-Modus** | Florian talks 70%+ of the time, learns nothing | Agenda rule: "Ihre Situation" = 15 min LISTENING. Prepare questions, not speeches. Source: Winning by Design, "Discovery calls need to be conversations" (2016) |

| **Kein Ask** | Meeting ends with "toll, wir melden uns" | Always have Primary Ask + Fallback Ask. Rehearse the transition sentence. |
| **Over-Engineering** | 3-hour prep for a 30-min coffee | Time-box: 30 min prep. If you need more, scope is wrong or stakes justify 45 min max. |
| **Assumption Bias** | Prep assumes pain points that don't exist | First 15 min = listening. Validate assumptions before pitching. Adjust in real-time. |
| **Tech-Demo ohne Kontext** | Showing AI capabilities nobody asked for | Demo only what maps to their stated problems. "Based on what you just told me..." |
