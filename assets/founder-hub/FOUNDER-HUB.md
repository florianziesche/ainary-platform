## Essential Founder How-To Guides

Actionable frameworks for every stage of your journey.

### 1. **How to Write a Pitch Deck**

**Outline:**
- **Introduction:** Why pitch decks matter (10 minutes to win or lose funding)
- **The 10 Core Slides:**
  1. Problem (What pain are you solving?)
  2. Solution (Your product in one sentence)
  3. Market Size (TAM, SAM, SOM)
  4. Product (Demo, screenshots, or prototype)
  5. Traction (Users, revenue, growth rate)
  6. Business Model (How you make money)
  7. Competition (Competitive landscape + your moat)
  8. Team (Why you're the ones to build this)
  9. Financials (3-5 year projections)
  10. Ask (How much you're raising + use of funds)
- **Design Principles:** Less text, more visuals. One idea per slide.
- **Common Mistakes:** Too many slides, lack of traction, weak team slide
- **Resources:** Sequoia Capital pitch template, Y Combinator guidelines

---

### 2. **How to Find Product-Market Fit**

**Outline:**
- **What is PMF?** Marc Andreessen's definition: "Being in a good market with a product that can satisfy that market"
- **The PMF Survey:** Sean Ellis's 40% rule (if >40% of users would be "very disappointed" if your product disappeared, you have PMF)
- **Metrics to Track:**
  - Retention curves (flatten = PMF)
  - NPS (Net Promoter Score)
  - Organic growth (word of mouth)
- **The Build-Measure-Learn Loop:**
  1. Ship an MVP
  2. Talk to users (minimum 30 interviews)
  3. Iterate based on feedback
  4. Repeat until retention improves
- **Case Studies:** Airbnb's photographer insight, Superhuman's PMF engine

**Outline:**
- **What is a Seed Round?** Typically $500Kâ€“$3M, 10â€“20% equity
- **Prerequisites:** MVP, initial traction, founding team
- **Step-by-Step Process:**

### 6. **How to Set Up Your Cap Table**

**Outline:**
- **What is a Cap Table?** A spreadsheet showing who owns what % of your company
- **Initial Setup:** Founders split equity (50/50, 60/40, etc. based on contribution)
- **Equity for Advisors:** 0.1â€“0.5% with 2-year vesting
- **Employee Option Pool:** Set aside 10â€“20% pre-raise
- **Post-Raise Cap Table:** Investors get 10â€“25%, founders dilute proportionally
- **Tools:** Carta, Pulley, AngelList Stack

### 7. **How to Negotiate Term Sheets**

**Outline:**
- **Key Terms to Understand:**
  - Valuation (pre-money vs. post-money)
  - Liquidation preferences (1x is standard)
  - Board composition (founder control vs. investor seats)
  - Pro-rata rights (investors can maintain ownership %)
  - Drag-along rights (force minority to sell if majority agrees)
- **What to Optimize For:**
  - Valuation (but not at all costs)
  - Investor quality (brand, network, expertise)
  - Control (board seats, protective provisions)
- **Red Flags:**
  - Participating preferred (avoid)
  - Ratchets (anti-dilution protection)
  - Overly restrictive vesting schedules
- **Negotiation Tactics:** Get multiple term sheets, use FOMO, lean on lawyer

### 8. **How to Build a Financial Model**

**Outline:**
- **Purpose:** Forecast revenue, expenses, cash flow for 3â€“5 years
- **Core Components:**
  1. Revenue model (pricing Ã— volume Ã— conversion)
  2. COGS (Cost of Goods Sold)
  3. Operating expenses (salaries, marketing, rent)
  4. Hiring plan
  5. Fundraising assumptions
- **Bottom-Up vs. Top-Down:**
  - Bottom-up: Start with unit economics (CAC, LTV)
  - Top-down: Start with TAM and market share assumptions
- **Scenarios:** Best case, base case, worst case
- **Tools:** Google Sheets, Excel, Causal, Finmark
- **Mistake:** Over-optimism (reduce your projections by 50%)

### 10. **How to Pivot Successfully**

- **Types of Pivots:**
  - Customer segment (B2B â†’ B2C)
  - Problem (same customers, different pain point)
  - Technology (rebuild on new stack)
  - Business model (SaaS â†’ marketplace)
- **When to Pivot:**
  - Flat or declining growth for 6+ months
  - No path to PMF despite iterations
  - Market changes (regulations, competitor, tech shift)
- **How to Execute:**
  1. Analyze what's not working (talk to churned users)
  2. Identify adjacent opportunities
  3. Test new direction with MVPs
  4. Communicate clearly to team, investors, customers

### 11. **How to Run Effective User Interviews**

**Outline:**
- **Why Interviews Matter:** Avoid building what nobody wants
- **Recruitment:** Friends, Twitter, cold outreach, UserTesting.com
- **The Script:**
  1. Warm-up (get them talking)
  2. Past behavior (how they solve problem today)
  3. Paint points (what frustrates them)
  4. Solution testing (show MVP, get feedback)
- **Dos:** Ask open-ended questions, listen more than talk, record sessions
- **Don'ts:** Pitch your idea, ask hypothetical questions, ignore negative feedback
- **Target:** 30 interviews minimum for patterns

- **What is Due Diligence?** Investor deep-dive into your company before writing check
- **What Investors Look At:**
  - Financial statements (revenue, burn rate)
  - Cap table (who owns what)
  - Customer contracts (MRR, churn)
  - IP/legal (do you own your code?)
  - HR (employee contracts, equity agreements)
- **Prep Your Data Room:**
  - Pitch deck
  - Financial model
  - Customer list + metrics
  - Legal docs (incorporation, IP assignments)
  - Code repo access (for technical DD)
- **Timeline:** 2â€“4 weeks for full due diligence
- **Red Flags:** Missing contracts, co-founder disputes, IP issues

  3. Demo/Discovery call
  4. Proposal/SOW
  5. Negotiation
  6. Close

### 20. **How to Exit Your Startup**

**Outline:**
- **Exit Types:**
  - Acquisition (most common for startups)
  - IPO (rare, only for unicorns)
  - Acqui-hire (team acquisition)
- **When to Exit:**
  - Inbound interest from acquirer
  - Market consolidation (roll-ups)
  - Founder burnout
  - Can't raise next round
- **The M&A Process:**
  1. Initial contact (acquirer reaches out or you engage banker)
  2. NDA + preliminary diligence
  3. LOI (Letter of Intent)
  4. Full due diligence (30â€“90 days)
  5. Final agreement + close
- **Valuation:** Typically 3â€“10x revenue for SaaS, varies widely
- **Post-Exit:** Earnouts, retention, integration
