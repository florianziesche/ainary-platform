## SYNTHESIS: The Discord Intelligence Playbook

### 1. How to Use Discord Communities for Deal Sourcing

**For VCs:**

**The traditional way (low yield):**
- Scroll #pitch channels
- Respond to cold DMs
- Wait for warm intros

**The intelligence way (high yield):**

1. **Lurk strategically in AI builder communities**  
   When a developer shares "Just hit $5K MRR with my AI tool" in #show-and-tell, that's a signal. They're 6-12 months from raising a seed round. Bookmark. Follow up in DMs. Build relationship now, before they're in market.

2. **Track the "I'm hiring" signal**  
   Founders hiring their first engineer = they have traction or just raised. Join #job-postings channels in startup Discords. Every hire announcement is a potential deal.

3. **Watch for repeat names across communities**  
   Someone active in LangChain Discord, Vercel Discord, and a founder community = they're building something AI + shipping fast. These are the operators you want to back.

4. **Join the right "rooms"**  
   Many Discords have private channels for verified VCs (proof of fund, LinkedIn, etc.). Get into those. The deal flow shared there is pre-filtered by other investors.

5. **Use the !looking-for channels**  
   Founders post "Looking for: intros to VCs in [sector]" ‚Äî that's inbound. Respond helpfully (not pitchy). Most VCs miss this because they don't read these channels.

**Real example:** A VC found a company in the Cursor Discord when the founder casually mentioned "We're using Cursor to build [product], hit $10K MRR." That throwaway comment became a seed check 3 months later.

---

### 2. How to Build Your Reputation in VC Discords

**The wrong way:**
- Join, immediately pitch yourself: "I'm raising $2M, here's my deck"
- Drop links to your blog posts
- Only show up when you need something

**The right way:**

**Month 1-2: Consume & Contribute**
- Read 10x more than you post
- Answer questions in areas you know deeply
- Share useful resources (articles, tools, data) without self-promotion

**Month 3-4: Build Visibility**
- Post thoughtful takes on recent news ("Here's why [trend] matters for early-stage VCs")
- Share your own data/learnings ("Analyzed 50 pre-seed decks, here's what stood out")
- Help others 1:1 in DMs (don't expect anything back)

**Month 5+: Leverage Reputation**
- Now when you say "I'm looking for X," people respond because they know you add value
- VCs start DMing you: "What do you think about [company]?"
- You get invited to private channels

**The key:** Reciprocity. Give 10x before you ask for 1x.

**Example:** A founder spent 3 months answering technical questions in an AI Discord. When they launched their product, 15 community members became paying customers + 2 VCs reached out to invest. They never "networked" ‚Äî they built trust.
