## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The venture capital market has stabilized after 3 years of decline, entering a new disciplined growth phase. **2025 saw $425B deployed globally** (up 30% YoY), marking the third-highest funding year ever—but with fundamental differences from the 2021 bubble. Capital concentration is extreme: 60% of invested capital went to just 629 companies raising $100M+ rounds. The AI sector alone captured **50% of all global venture funding ($211B)**, creating the largest capital concentration around a single technology wave in VC history.

**Key structural shifts:**
- **Mega-rounds are back, seed is stagnant:** Average deal size jumped to $20.1M (from $14.1M), but total deal count remains suppressed
- **Late-stage dominance:** 47% of capital now flows to late-stage (up 11% YoY), median late-stage round: $45M (was $30M)
- **U.S. market share surge:** 64% of global capital (up from 56% in 2024, vs. 47-48% in 2019-2023)
- **Exit market thawing:** M&A at second-highest levels on record; IPO window opening selectively
- **Discipline over hype:** Down rounds normalized, profitability demanded, "growth at all costs" dead

**Bottom line:** The market has bifurcated. Elite AI companies command unprecedented valuations (OpenAI $500B, SpaceX $800B, Anthropic $183B) while most startups face a higher bar for funding. This creates asymmetric opportunities for positioned investors and operators who understand where the consensus is wrong.

### Time Between Rounds

- **Seed → Series A:** 18-24 months (extended from 12-18 months in 2021)
- **Series A → B:** 24-36 months (was 18-24 months)
- **Later stages:** 18-30 months (variable; elite companies raising opportunistically)

**Insight:** Runway expectations have increased. Investors demand 24+ months of runway at current burn. Companies that raised in 2021-22 at high valuations are hitting inflection points in 2026, forcing either substantial down rounds or shutdowns.

### Down Rounds / Flat Rounds Percentage

- **2023:** ~30% of rounds were down/flat (peak correction)
- **2024:** ~25% down/flat (stabilization)
- **2025:** ~15-20% down/flat (improvement, but still elevated vs. pre-2022)

**Context:** Down rounds peaked during correction but remain common. Many 2021-vintage unicorns have taken "inside rounds" at flat valuations to extend runway. This overhang will persist through 2026-27 as companies grow into inflated valuations.

### IPO Window Status

**Assessment: SELECTIVELY OPEN (Improving)**

- **2024:** Modest recovery (Arm, Instacart, Klaviyo, ServiceTitan successful)
- **2025:** Window widened for high-quality companies
  - Digital health led IPO activity
  - Fintech: Chime ($10B), Circle ($6.9B) went public
  - **BUT:** Only 44% of exit value via IPOs (56% M&A)—reversed from 2021 (80% IPO / 20% M&A)
- **2026 outlook:** Selective for profitable/near-profitable companies with >$200M revenue and strong growth

**Bottleneck:** Public market appetite exists for elite companies, but the bar is high. Most unicorns still trapped, waiting for growth to justify valuations.

### M&A Activity

**Status: RECORD LEVELS (Second-highest year ever)**

- **2025 M&A:** Second-highest year on record; U.S. M&A activity exceeded even 2021
- **Largest deal:** Google acquired Wiz (cybersecurity) for $32B—largest VC-backed acquisition ever
- **Q2 2025:** 205 fintech M&A deals; Coinbase acquired Deribit for $2.9B
- **Sectors leading M&A:** Cybersecurity, fintech, digital health, AI infrastructure

**Strategic shift:** M&A is now the **primary exit path** for most startups. Mega-acquirers (Google, Microsoft, AWS, Salesforce) are buying category leaders rather than building. This trend favors "second-tier" winners who can be acquired for $500M-$5B.

### LP Sentiment Toward Venture

**Assessment: CAUTIOUSLY OPTIMISTIC (Selective Flight to Quality)**

**Negative signals:**
- LP commitments to VC fell dramatically 2022-2024
- **First-time fund fundraising collapsed:** Only $3.6B raised in 2025 (down 85% from 2021's $24B)
- Emerging managers (<$250M funds) captured only 20% of capital in 2024 (245 funds, $15B)

**Positive signals:**
- Established firms raising large funds successfully (concentration of LP dollars)
- Corporate VC doubled in H1 2025 ($129B across 2,474 deals)
- Improving exit environment restoring LP confidence
- "When there's positive liquidity, more money goes into venture"—cycle beginning

**Synthesis:** LPs are committing capital, but **only to proven managers**. The VC industry itself is consolidating—power law now applies to fund managers, not just companies. Opportunity exists for small, specialized funds (<€50M) that can access deals large funds ignore, but raising capital as a first-time manager is nearly impossible.

## SECTION 3: CONTRARIAN BETS — What the Crowd is WRONG About

### 5 Things VC Consensus Believes (That Might Be Wrong)

#### 1. **CONSENSUS: "AI will create winner-take-all markets dominated by foundation model companies"**

**CONTRARIAN TAKE:** Foundation models will commoditize into public infrastructure (like Linux), and value will accrue to application layer + specialized models.

**Why consensus might be wrong:**
