### TIER 1 — Synthesized Intelligence (200 words)

**Consensus:** Technology—especially AI—is the dominant force shaping the next decade. Regulatory capture and "doomerism" are existential threats to innovation. The best founders have specific knowledge, high agency, and move faster than incumbents. San Francisco's decline is a civilizational warning sign.

**Disagreements:** 
- **Timelines:** Altman says AGI in 3-5 years; Andreessen more skeptical of consciousness but bullish on capabilities.
- **Geography:** Rabois and Sacks abandoned SF for Miami/Austin; Tan is fighting to save SF; pg doesn't care where you are if you're building.
- **Crypto:** Balaji is maximalist; Sacks is engaged; Andreessen backs it as tech freedom; most others lukewarm.
- **Regulation:** Spectrum from Andreessen's "all regulation is bad" to Altman's "we need thoughtful AI governance."

**Hidden Signal:** This group is deeply **status quo threatened**. They rose in Web 2.0 and worry the next generation won't respect their playbook. Their anti-regulation stance is partly principled, partly self-interested (protecting portfolio companies). The most interesting divergence is **Naval's detachment**—he's the only one not actively empire-building, which makes his takes purer but less grounded.

**What They're Missing:** International perspectives, climate constraints, inequality feedback loops, and what happens when their "build" ethos collides with real-world political economy.

### TIER 2 — Synthesized Intelligence (200 words)

**Consensus:** AI is reshaping every investment category, but **how** is fiercely debated. Infrastructure vs. application layer; vertical vs. horizontal; open source vs. closed. Consumer investing is under existential pressure—no one has found the TikTok successor. SaaS metrics still matter but need updating for AI-native products. Media is the new way to build a fund brand (Packy, Harry, Byrne).

**Disagreements:**
- **Consumer vs. B2B:** Sarah Tavel and Rex Woodbury focused on consumer, but consensus is B2B is where the money is.
- **AI value capture:** Sarah Guo bullish on applications; Tomasz Tunguz says infrastructure will win; Josh Wolfe says both are wrong—edge cases will dominate.
- **Crypto:** Fred Wilson still believes; most others moved on.
- **Fund strategy:** Solo GPs (Turner, Semil, Nikhil) vs. platform funds (Benchmark, Lux, a16z).

**Hidden Signal:** The **media → venture capital pipeline** is now established. Harry, Packy, Byrne, and Rex have proven content is deal flow. This tier is increasingly **founder-as-investor**—operators tired of institutional VC constraints going solo (Sarah Guo, Tomasz Tunguz). 

**What They're Missing:** This group is very US-centric. International opportunities (especially LatAm, Africa, India at scale) are blind spots. Also: very few have real expertise in AI/ML—they're pattern-matching, not building.

### TIER 4 — Synthesized Intelligence (200 words)

**Consensus:** **Operators make better investors** because they understand execution, not just pattern-matching. The best founders want investors who've "been there" — fundraising, hiring, firing, pivoting, scaling. This group is deeply skeptical of "tourist VCs" who've never operated. They believe **capital is commoditized; operational value-add is the differentiation**.

**Disagreements:**
- **VC vs. Bootstrap:** Sahil Lavingia says don't raise; Jason Lemkin says SaaS needs VC; most others case-by-case.
- **SaaS playbook:** Jason Lemkin dogmatic about SaaS metrics; Andrew Chen says consumer/marketplace is different; Lenny Rachitsky says PM principles are universal.
- **Media vs. Investing:** Lenny and Ryan Hoover building media empires; Tracy Young and April Underwood building traditional funds.

**Hidden Signal:** The **content-to-capital flywheel is real**. Lenny Rachitsky built a newsletter → now angel investing with massive deal flow. Jason Lemkin built SaaStr community → fund. Ryan Hoover built Product Hunt → fund. This is the playbook for operators wanting to transition to investing.

**Florian's Opportunity:** This tier is the **most accessible for networking**. They're not Tier 1 gatekeepers. They're building, and they value operators. Engage with their content, share your operational lessons, and build relationships. When you're ready to raise or angel invest, this is your cohort.

**What They're Missing:** They can be **overly prescriptive** based on their own experience. Jason Lemkin's SaaS playbook doesn't work for AI-native companies. Andrew Chen's marketplace frameworks don't work for vertical SaaS. Avoid blindly following their playbooks—extract principles, adapt to your context.
