Perspective

What I believe about AI and industry

The $11 trillion global labor market is 24× the size of the $450 billion software market. The real AI opportunity isn't better chatbots — it's replacing entire workflows in industries where expertise is scarce and labor is expensive. Manufacturing. Legal. Healthcare. Construction.

Vertical AI beats horizontal AI. The moat isn't model performance — it's domain knowledge encoded into systems. The best AI companies are built by operators who've worked in the industries they're automating, not by generalists watching from the sideline.

I've spent five years building AI products for these industries — first as a startup CEO, then scaling a computer vision SaaS to enterprise customers across automotive and manufacturing. The pattern is clear: operators build better AI. And the bridge between European industrial depth and American market scale is an arbitrage most people can't access.
