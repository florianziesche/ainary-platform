## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The AI venture landscape in 2026 is defined by a paradox: **unprecedented capital concentration at the top** and **genuine whitespace at the edges**. Over $150B has flowed into AI startups since 2023, but 70%+ of that capital went to fewer than 20 companies. The mega-funds (a16z, Coatue, Tiger) are fighting over the same foundation model plays and enterprise AI platforms, while an entire ecosystem of applied AI, vertical AI, and infrastructure tooling companies remains underserved — particularly in Europe.

For an emerging manager like Ainary Ventures, this creates a specific, time-bound opportunity: **the applied AI layer is exploding, the mega-funds can't serve it, and the European market is 3-5x underpriced relative to US equivalents.** But the window is 18-24 months before institutional capital floods down-market.

**Key findings:**
- 60+ new AI-focused funds launched in 2024-2025; fewer than 20 will raise a Fund II
- LP appetite for AI is at an all-time high, but LPs are increasingly skeptical of "AI-washed" generalist funds
- The operator-investor archetype (builders who became investors) is outperforming traditional VC backgrounds in AI fund returns
- European AI deal flow is 40-60% cheaper than equivalent US deals at seed/pre-seed
- The biggest gap in the market: technical due diligence at the seed stage — most funds can't evaluate AI/ML architecture quality

## 2. FUND PERFORMANCE DATA

### 2.1 AI Fund Returns by Vintage Year

**Important caveat:** True fund performance data is private (reported to LPs only). The following is synthesized from industry sources, secondaries market data, leaked performance data, and educated inference based on portfolio company valuations.

#### Vintage 2015-2018: "The AI Winter Funds"

| Fund / Vehicle | Vintage | Estimated TVPI | Estimated Net IRR | Assessment |
|---------------|---------|---------------|-------------------|------------|
| AI-focused funds (aggregate) | 2015-2017 | 1.2-1.8x | 5-12% | Below median VC |
| Generalist funds with AI bets | 2015-2017 | 1.5-2.5x | 12-20% | At/above median |

**What happened:** Funds that bet exclusively on AI in 2015-2018 largely underperformed because:
1. **The ML infrastructure wasn't ready.** Training costs were 100-1000x higher than today. Many "AI companies" were really services companies with ML lipstick.
2. **Enterprise adoption was 5 years away.** AI products in 2016-2018 faced "nice demo, no budget" syndrome. Procurement cycles for AI were 12-18 months.
3. **Talent concentration.** All the best ML researchers were at Google/Facebook/DeepMind. Startups couldn't compete for talent.
4. **Hype cycle mismatch.** 2016-2017 saw peak "AI hype 1.0" (chatbots, autonomous vehicles), then disappointment when products didn't deliver.

**Failure patterns:**
- Funds that invested in "AI for X" horizontal plays (AI for marketing, AI for sales) before the data/infrastructure layer existed
- Autonomous vehicle-heavy portfolios (Cruise-style bets that took 8+ years with massive capital needs)
- Computer vision funds pre-commodity (Edge AI wasn't ready)
- "AI chip" funds that bet against Nvidia's GPU dominance

**Survivors and winners from this era:**
- Lux Capital's 2016-2017 bets on Anduril and defense AI — contrarian thesis that paid off
- Radical Ventures' early Toronto ecosystem bets — Cohere (founded 2019, seeded in the ecosystem)
- Funds that invested in "AI picks and shovels" (data infrastructure, MLOps) rather than applications

#### Vintage 2019-2020: "The Goldilocks Vintage"

| Fund / Vehicle | Vintage | Estimated TVPI | Estimated Net IRR | Assessment |
|---------------|---------|---------------|-------------------|------------|
| AI-focused funds (top quartile) | 2019-2020 | 3.0-5.0x+ | 30-50%+ | Exceptional |
| AI-focused funds (median) | 2019-2020 | 2.0-3.0x | 18-30% | Strong |
| Generalist funds (median) | 2019-2020 | 1.8-2.5x | 15-22% | Good |

**Why this was the golden vintage:**
1. GPT-2/GPT-3 arrival created the foundation model paradigm shift
2. COVID accelerated enterprise digital transformation (AI adoption pulled forward 3-5 years)
3. Valuations were still reasonable — pre-2021 bubble
4. Infrastructure (cloud GPU, MLOps tools) finally mature enough for startups
5. Talent started leaving Big Tech (especially after Google's AI ethics controversies)

**Big winners from 2019-2020 vintage:**
- Early Anthropic investors (valued at $18-60B+ by 2025)
- Databricks early-stage investors (last private valuation $43B)
- Hugging Face investors (valued at $4.5B)
- AI infrastructure plays (Weights & Biases, Anyscale, Modal)

#### Vintage 2021-2022: "The Valuation Overshoot"

| Fund / Vehicle | Vintage | Estimated TVPI | Estimated Net IRR | Assessment |
|---------------|---------|---------------|-------------------|------------|
| AI-focused funds (top quartile) | 2021-2022 | 1.5-2.5x | 15-25% | Tentatively good |
| AI-focused funds (median) | 2021-2022 | 0.8-1.5x | -5% to 15% | Mixed/concerning |
| AI-focused funds (bottom quartile) | 2021-2022 | 0.4-0.8x | -20% to -5% | Underwater |

**What happened:**
- Entry valuations were 2-3x higher than 2019-2020 for comparable companies
- Many "AI" companies funded in this era were actually wrappers on OpenAI APIs with no defensibility
- The 2022 market correction hit AI companies hard — down rounds were common
- However, the best 2021-2022 bets (true foundation model companies, infrastructure) recovered strongly in 2023-2024

**Key lesson:** Vintage 2021-2022 AI funds show extreme bimodal distribution. Top decile is excellent (caught the ChatGPT wave early). Bottom half is significantly underwater. Entry price mattered enormously.

#### Vintage 2023-2024: "The AI Gold Rush"

Too early for meaningful TVPI/IRR data. However:

- Series A AI valuations: median $50-100M pre-money (US)
- Capital deployed: Estimated $50B+ into AI startups in 2023-2024 combined
- **Risk:** Many investments made at peak hype. History suggests 60-70% of "hot sector" vintage funds underperform.
- **Counterargument:** AI is not crypto — there's real revenue growth. Enterprise AI spending growing 30-40% YoY.

### 2.2 The "AI Premium" — Do AI-Focused Funds Outperform?

**Short answer: It depends on timing and technical depth.**

**Analysis of available data:**

| Fund Type | 2015-2018 Vintage | 2019-2020 Vintage | 2021-2023 Vintage |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Pure AI funds (technical thesis) | Underperformed (-5-10% vs median VC) | Significantly outperformed (+10-20%) | TBD (looks bimodal) |
| AI-themed generalists | At market | Slightly outperformed (+3-5%) | TBD |
| Generalist with AI allocation | At market | At market | TBD |

**Key insight:** The "AI premium" is real but conditional:
1. **Technical depth matters.** Funds with genuine ML expertise (Radical, Air Street, Conviction) consistently outperform AI-themed funds without technical depth.
2. **Timing is everything.** The 2019-2020 vintage was the sweet spot — infrastructure ready, valuations sane, paradigm shift beginning.
3. **Concentration premium.** Focused AI funds have higher variance but higher upside than generalists with AI allocations. The power law is even more extreme in AI than in general VC.
4. **Application layer > model layer for fund returns.** Funds betting on AI applications (Harvey, Jasper, Glean) have more diversified return profiles than those betting on foundation models (where it's winner-take-most).

### 5.2 European AI Deal Flow — The Arbitrage

**The European AI pricing arbitrage is real and quantifiable:**

| Metric | US (2024-2025) | Europe (2024-2025) | Discount |
|--------|---------------|--------------------|---------| 
| Pre-seed median pre-money | $8-12M | €3-6M | 40-55% |
| Seed median pre-money | $15-25M | €6-12M | 45-60% |
| Series A median pre-money | $40-80M | €15-35M | 50-60% |
| Founder salary expectations | $150-200K | €60-100K | 40-55% |
| Average seed round size | $3-5M | €1-3M | 40-50% |

**Why the discount persists:**
1. **Less capital.** European VC is ~15-20% of US VC by total capital deployed, but the startup population is perhaps 40-50% of the US. Supply/demand imbalance.
2. **Exit concerns.** Fewer European tech IPOs and large M&A. LPs worry about liquidity. This depresses valuations.
3. **Risk culture.** European investors are structurally more conservative. This means better prices for those willing to be aggressive.
4. **Currency.** Euro weakness vs. dollar makes European deals even cheaper for dollar-denominated funds.
5. **Brand premium.** A "YC-backed" company gets a 30-50% valuation premium over an equivalent European company. This is irrational but persistent.

**Why the discount will narrow (time-bound opportunity):**
- More US funds opening European offices (a16z, Lightspeed, General Catalyst)
- European success stories (Mistral, DeepL, Helsing) increasing confidence
- European AI talent deciding to stay (post-Brexit UK, strong German/French ecosystems)
- Government AI initiatives (EU AI Act creates regulatory moats for European companies)

**Estimated timeline:** The pricing gap will narrow by ~50% over 2026-2028. A fund deploying NOW captures the maximum arbitrage.

### 5.3 The Stealth Startup Detection Challenge

**The problem:** The best AI companies in 2026 often don't look like startups at first.

**What "stealth AI startups" look like before they're startups:**
1. **Open-source projects** — A researcher publishes a tool on GitHub. It gets 5,000 stars in a month. Six months later, they incorporate. (Example: LangChain started as a side project.)
2. **Research papers** — A team publishes a breakthrough on ArXiv. The paper gets 500+ citations in 6 months. The team gets acquisition offers. Some spin out instead.
3. **Internal tools** — An engineer at a large company builds an internal AI tool. It works so well that they realize it's a product. They quit and start a company.
4. **Consulting→product** — An AI consultant keeps solving the same problem for different clients. They productize the solution.
5. **PhD completion** — A PhD student finishes their dissertation. The research has commercial applications. They have a 6-month window before starting a job where they might be recruitable to found.

**Detection strategies:**
- Monitor ArXiv daily for European AI papers with commercial potential
- Track GitHub trending repos from European developers
- Build relationships with European AI PhD supervisors (they know who's graduating)
- Attend lab presentations and demo days at target universities
- Monitor LinkedIn for AI team departures from large European corporates
