## KEY EVIDENCE

### 1. Electoral Lab (France) — THE CLOSEST COMPETITOR 🚨
**Le Monde, 22.02.2026** (YESTERDAY): Paul Brounais runs Electoral Lab, a political communications agency using AI for municipal election candidates in **60 French towns** (pop. 250 to 220,000). This is our EXACT market, one country over.
- **Difference:** They're a campaign agency using AI as a tool. We're building a self-service intelligence platform.
- **Implication:** Market validation. If France has it, Germany will want it.

### 2. US Market — Mature but Federal-Focused
| Player | Focus | Revenue | Relevance to us |

**Key finding:** US tools are either (a) federal/state-level policy tracking (FiscalNote, Quorum), (b) narrow single-function (CallTime = fundraising, Quiller = copywriting), or (c) consulting wrapped in tech. **NONE do municipal-level candidate intelligence with evidence grading.** **[B1]** Multiple sources

### 3. European Market — Nearly Empty
- **No dedicated municipal election intelligence platform found** for Germany, UK, or EU.
- **Palantir** sells to UK local councils (£670M total UK government contracts), but for **service delivery**, not elections. **[A2]** Guardian, Novara Media, 2025-2026
- **EU 2024 Parliament elections**: One academic paper (Tosi et al., 2025) documented AI chatbot use — experimental, not commercial. **[A2]** SAGE Journals
- **Germany specifically**: pollytix, BUTTER, Cosmonauts & Kings (agencies) use AI internally, but no SaaS product for candidates. **[B2]** Our COMPETITORS.md research

### 4. AI in Political Campaigns — The Current State
- **Harvard Ash Center (Apr 2025)**: "AI's influence on politics has evolved... new tools available to politicians." But focused on national level. **[A2]**
- **American Prospect (Oct 2025)**: "The most impactful uses of AI in the 2026 midterms may not be known until 2027." Campaigns use AI secretly. **[B1]**
- **AEI (Dec 2025)**: AI chatbots shifted voter preferences 3.9 points on 100-point scale — 4x more effective than traditional ads. This is persuasion, not intelligence. **[A2]**
- **LSE Public Policy Review (Nov 2024)**: Campaigns train chatbots on LLMs to answer voter questions. Content generation, not analysis. **[B1]**
