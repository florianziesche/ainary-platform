### Answer to: How will CEN/CENELEC define 'accuracy' for AI agents, and who's influencing these standards?

**Key Findings:**

- **CEN/CENELEC is accelerating AI standards development but accuracy definition remains undefined** [E] — The joint Technical Boards adopted "exceptional measures" in October 2025 to deliver standards supporting the AI Act, with summer 2025 targeting implementation requirements (https://www.cencenelec.eu/news-events/news/2025/brief-news/2025-10-23-ai-standardization/). However, no specific accuracy definition for AI agents found in available drafts.

- **EU AI Act requires 'accuracy' not 'calibration' but lacks operational definition** [E] — Article 15 mandates accuracy requirements for high-risk AI systems [S14], but the Commission's July 2025 draft Guidelines for GPAI models focus on transparency rather than accuracy metrics (https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/ai-act-explorer/).

- **Working groups show academic influence over BigTech lobbying** [I] — Key editors include Rania Wazir (leiwand.ai, Austria) for transparency standards and standards experts Koen Holtman (AI Standards Lab) and Tekla Emborg (Future of Life Institute) (https://jtc21.eu/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/CEN-CENELEC-JTC21-AI-Standards-Complete-Detailed-Overview.pdf). No evidence found of direct BigTech working group membership.

- **Meta actively resists EU compliance frameworks** [E] — Meta "rejects GPAI Code" due to "innovation impact concerns" and fears compliance could "diminish European AI innovation," echoing broader tensions with EU regulators over Digital Markets Act (https://digital.nemko.com/news/meta-refuses-gpai-code).

- **BigTech promotes self-regulation over mandatory standards** [I] — Google emphasizes "AI Principles" for "socially beneficial AI" while Microsoft advocates for "regulations that respect human rights" rather than specific accuracy metrics (https://transcend.io/blog/big-tech-ai-governance). This suggests preference for flexible principles over hard calibration requirements.

- **Standards timeline creates urgency for CT-016 positioning** [E] — CEN-CENELEC JTC21 standards are in "Final Vote to Publish Stage" with summer 2025 implementation targets, while Commission Guidelines are planned for Q2 2026 (https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai).

**Evidence Quality:**
- **Strongest source:** Official CEN-CENELEC announcements and EU AI Act text provide authoritative timeline and legal requirements
- **Weakest point:** No direct evidence of accuracy definition methodology or specific BigTech lobbying positions on calibration vs. other metrics  
- **What's missing:** Actual draft standard text, working group meeting minutes, BigTech position papers on accuracy metrics

**So What:** CEN/CENELEC has not yet defined 'accuracy' for AI agents, creating a window for CT-016 to influence whether calibration becomes the standard. Academic experts currently dominate working groups while BigTech resists mandatory frameworks, suggesting calibration requirements could become mandatory if positioned as essential for AI Act compliance.
