### Answer to: What's the actual liability exposure for uncalibrated agent decisions, and will insurance companies require calibration?

**Key Findings:**

- **Insurance exclusions accelerating** [E]: Major insurers are "pulling back from AI liability" with new exclusions for "intelligent agents," automated decision-making, and technology errors, while adding AI-specific endorsements with sublimits for regulatory penalties (https://www.metropolitanrisk.com/major-insurers-are-pulling-back-from-ai-liability/).

- **State liability expansion creating exposure** [E]: 2025-2026 state AI bills are creating "novel pathways to liability" and "expanding liability exposures for all businesses incorporating AI solutions," with underwriters now considering "AI-specific policy terms" (https://www.wiley.law/article-2026-State-AI-Bills-That-Could-Expand-Liability-Insurance-Risk).

- **D&O coverage gaps emerging** [E]: Directors face "unrecognized liabilities under the false pretense that such risks are fully insured under traditional D&O liability policies" due to AI exclusions, particularly where AI is used in "HR hiring algorithms or board-level strategic decisions" (https://corpgov.law.harvard.edu/2025/09/22/the-hidden-c-suite-risk-of-ai-failures/).

- **Tort liability already applies** [E]: AI harms fall under existing tort law without requiring new legislation, with courts finding that "the Integrator who is in control of the risks connected with the AI" bears first-instance liability when customers act on faulty AI information (https://www.hfw.com/insights/legal-liability-for-ai-driven-decisions-when-ai-gets-it-wrong-who-can-you-turn-to/).

- **Calibration creates liability reduction mechanism** [I]: Given that verbalized confidence shows 42% error calibration vs 27.3% for consistency methods [S8], and overconfidence appears in 84% of scenarios [S8], insurers could require calibration as a risk mitigation control similar to MFA requirements that led to denied payouts (https://thehackernews.com/2026/02/identity-cyber-scores-new-metric.html).

- **Human oversight legally mandated** [E]: EU AI Act Article 14 requires human oversight [S14], but human vigilance drops 20-50% after 30 minutes monitoring automated systems [S15], creating a liability gap that calibration could address.

- **No direct calibration requirements yet** [J]: While cyber insurers use AI for risk scoring (https://www.tcs.com/what-we-do/industries/insurance/white-paper/ai-overcoming-cyber-insurance-industry-challenges), no evidence found of specific calibration metric requirements in current policies.

**Evidence Quality:**
- **Strongest source**: Wiley law firm analysis of 2025-2026 state legislation showing concrete liability expansion
- **Weakest point**: No documented cases of calibration-specific insurance requirements or court decisions hinging on confidence metrics
- **What's missing**: Actual insurance policy language, actuarial models pricing uncalibrated AI risk, court cases where calibration was dispositive

**So What:** Insurance-driven calibration adoption is imminent but not yet implemented—insurers are excluding AI liability while state laws expand exposure, creating a coverage gap that calibration requirements will likely fill as a quantifiable risk control. Companies should implement calibration proactively before insurers mandate it as a policy condition.
