# Part 2: Pattern Analysis ‚Äî What Actually Works

## A. Content Format Patterns

### What formats generate the most engagement?

**Twitter/X:**
- **Threads (3-6 tweets):** The #1 format for growth. Top performers like Sahil Bloom, Andrew Chen, and Li Jin built audiences primarily through threads. Tight threads (3-6 tweets) outperform long ones (10+).
- **One-liner hot takes:** Paul Graham, Naval, Keith Rabois ‚Äî short, aphoristic tweets that invite debate. High reply-to-impression ratio.
- **Memes + analysis combo:** Turner Novak, Trung Phan ‚Äî humor gets the share, analysis gets the follow.
- **Quote-tweets with commentary:** Adding your take to others' content is the easiest entry point.

**LinkedIn:**
- **Text-only posts (150-300 words):** Justin Welsh proved this ‚Äî short, punchy, one idea per post. LinkedIn's algorithm favors text posts over links.
- **Carousels:** High save rates, especially for frameworks and step-by-step guides.
- **Personal story ‚Üí lesson format:** The #1 viral format. "I did X, learned Y, here's what it means for you."
- **Numbered lists:** "7 things I learned..." ‚Äî scannable, shareable, high comment rates.

**Substack/Newsletter:**
- **Deep dives (3,000-10,000 words):** Packy McCormick, Mario Gabriele, Ben Thompson ‚Äî quality over frequency.
- **Framework + data + storytelling:** The magic trio. Lenny Rachitsky combines all three.
- **Weekly cadence:** 1-2x per week is the sweet spot. More burns out the writer; less loses subscriber attention.

**YouTube/Podcast:**
- **Long-form (1-3 hours):** Acquired, All-In, 20VC ‚Äî deep context wins on audio/video.
- **Clips (60-90 seconds):** Repurposed from long-form for Twitter/LinkedIn/TikTok.

### ‚Üí **Florian should:** Start with LinkedIn text posts (150-300 words) and Twitter threads (3-6 tweets). Add a weekly newsletter within 90 days.

## B. Post Length Patterns

**LinkedIn (optimal):**
- Hook: 1-2 lines (visible before "see more")
- Body: 8-15 short lines with line breaks
- Total: 150-300 words for regular posts
- Long posts (500-800 words) work for frameworks/stories, but less frequently

**Twitter (optimal):**
- Single tweets: Under 200 characters for shareability
- Threads: 3-6 tweets, each self-contained
- Thread tweet 1 (hook): Must be compelling enough to earn the click-through

### ‚Üí **Florian should:** Keep LinkedIn posts under 250 words. Twitter threads at exactly 5 tweets. One idea per post, always.

## D. Hook Patterns ‚Äî How Top Posts Start

Based on analysis of viral posts from the top 100 voices, these are the dominant hook patterns:

### 1. **The Contrarian Opener** (Paul Graham, Chamath, Keith Rabois)
> "Most people think X. They're wrong."  
> "Unpopular opinion: [bold claim]"  
> "Everyone is talking about AI agents. Nobody is talking about [this]."

### 2. **The Personal Story Hook** (Sahil Bloom, Harry Stebbings, Justin Welsh)
> "I almost went broke at 27."  
> "3 years ago I left my PE job with no plan."  
> "I was wrong about [X] for 5 years."

### 3. **The Number Hook** (Andrew Chen, Tomasz Tunguz, Lenny Rachitsky)
> "I analyzed 500 startups. Here's what the top 1% do differently."  
> "7 things I learned from losing $2M in my first fund."  
> "93% of VCs don't return their fund. Here's why."

### 4. **The Framework Hook** (Li Jin, Elad Gil, Packy McCormick)
> "There are only 3 types of [X]. Here's which one wins."  
> "The [Name] Framework for [desired outcome]."  
> "A simple mental model that changed how I think about [X]."

### 5. **The Authority/Credibility Hook** (Alex Hormozi, Lenny Rachitsky)
> "I've built 3 companies to $10M+. Here's what nobody tells you."  
> "After interviewing 200 PMs, here's what the best ones do."

### 6. **The Curiosity Gap** (Shaan Puri, Sam Parr)
> "The most underrated business model of 2025."  
> "I just found the most interesting startup I've seen in years."  
> "This is the playbook nobody shares publicly."

### ‚Üí **Florian should:** Rotate between these 6 hook types. Lead with contrarian + personal story hooks (highest engagement for smaller accounts). Never start with a generic statement.

## E. CTA Patterns ‚Äî How Top Posts End

### 2. **The Follow CTA** (growth-focused)
> "If you found this useful, follow me for more on [topic]."  
> "I write about [topic] every day. Follow for more."

### 3. **The Repost/Share CTA** (reach-focused)
> "Repost this if you agree ‚ôªÔ∏è"  
> "Share this with a founder who needs to hear it."

### 4. **The Newsletter/Funnel CTA** (conversion-focused)
> "I go deeper on this in my newsletter. Link in bio/comments."  
> "Full breakdown in this week's issue of [newsletter name]."

### ‚Üí **Florian should:** Use Question CTAs on 70% of posts (drives comments = algorithmic boost). Soft follow CTAs on 20%. Newsletter CTAs on 10%.

## G. Formatting Patterns

### LinkedIn best practices (from analyzing Justin Welsh, Hormozi, Lenny):
```
Hook line (grabs attention)

‚Üì Short line breaks (creates white space)

‚Üì One idea per line

‚Üì Bold key phrases sparingly

‚Üì Numbered lists for frameworks

‚Üì End with question or CTA
```

### Twitter best practices:
```
Tweet 1: Hook (strongest line, promise of value)
Tweet 2-4: Deliver value
Tweet 5: Recap + CTA

Use: ‚Üí bullets, numbers, and emojis sparingly
```

### What NOT to do:
- ‚ùå Wall of text (no line breaks)
- ‚ùå Starting with "I'm excited to announce..."
