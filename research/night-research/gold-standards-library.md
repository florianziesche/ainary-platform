### Common Structure / Pattern

All top research reports share:
- **Executive Summary** (1 page max) â€” key finding + "so what"
- **Context / Background** â€” why this matters now
- **Methodology** â€” how the research was conducted (transparency)
- **Findings** â€” organized by theme, with data visualizations
- **Analysis / Implications** â€” second-order effects, what it means
- **Recommendations** â€” specific, actionable
- **Appendices** â€” detailed data, methodology notes
- **Pyramid Principle (McKinsey):** Lead with the conclusion, then support with grouped arguments, then detail

### 5 Template Rules

1. **Answer first.** Open with your conclusion in the first paragraph. Busy readers may never reach page 3.
2. **MECE your sections.** Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive â€” no overlaps, no gaps in your argument structure.
3. **Every claim has a source.** Link or footnote every data point. Unsourced claims destroy credibility.
4. **Visualize, don't describe.** If a finding can be a chart, make it a chart. Data tables for precision; charts for patterns.
5. **"So what?" on every page.** Every section must answer why the reader should care. If you can't state the implication, cut it.

4. **Tailor to the role.** One-size-fits-all resumes don't work. Reorder bullets and adjust emphasis for each application.
5. **Format for ATS + humans.** Use standard section headers (Education, Experience, Skills). Avoid tables, columns, graphics, or unusual fonts that ATS can't parse. PDF for humans, .docx for ATS.

### Key Metrics Every VC Fund Model Must Include

- **TVPI** (Total Value to Paid-In Capital) â€” total value from distributions + residual value / paid-in capital
- **DPI** (Distributions to Paid-In Capital) â€” actual cash returned / paid-in capital
- **RVPI** (Residual Value to Paid-In Capital) â€” unrealized value / paid-in capital
- **MOIC** (Multiple on Invested Capital) â€” total value / invested capital (deal-level)
- **IRR** (Internal Rate of Return) â€” time-weighted return; standard for LP reporting
- **Management Fee** â€” typically 2% of committed capital
- **Carried Interest** â€” typically 20% of profits above hurdle rate
- **J-Curve** â€” visualization of early losses before returns

2. **Foresight.is Rolling Fund Model** â€” Specialized for rolling fund structures. https://foresight.is/rolling-fund-template/
3. **OpenVC: How to Model a Venture Capital Fund** â€” Step-by-step tutorial on building a fund model. https://www.openvc.app/blog/how-to-model-a-venture-capital-fund
4. **Carta Fund Performance Guide** â€” Explains TVPI, DPI, IRR, MOIC with context. https://carta.com/learn/private-funds/management/fund-performance/
5. **Graph Advisors: Free VC Fund Metrics Template** â€” Free calculator for TVPI, MOIC, DPI. https://graphadvisors.gumroad.com/l/VC-Fund-Metrics-TVPI-MOIC-DPI-Template | Explainer: https://graphadvisors.substack.com/p/understanding-key-vc-fund-performance

### Common Structure / Pattern

```
TAB 1: ASSUMPTIONS
- Fund size, management fee %, carry %, hurdle rate
- Investment period, fund life
- Number of investments, average check size
- Reserve ratio for follow-ons
- Graduation rates between rounds
- Expected dilution per round
- Exit timing assumptions

TAB 2: PORTFOLIO CONSTRUCTION
- Investment-by-investment or cohort-based modeling
- Initial investment â†’ follow-on allocations
- Ownership at entry â†’ dilution through rounds â†’ ownership at exit

TAB 3: EXIT SCENARIOS
- Exit multiples by outcome tier
- Exit timing (year 3-10)
- Distribution waterfall

TAB 4: FUND ECONOMICS (LP VIEW)
- Capital calls schedule
- Management fees
- Distributions timeline
- DPI, TVPI, RVPI, IRR by year

TAB 5: GP ECONOMICS
- Management company budget
- Carry accrual and distribution
- GP commit and returns

TAB 6: SENSITIVITY ANALYSIS
- Vary: graduation rates, exit multiples, dilution
- Show impact on TVPI and IRR
```

### 5 Template Rules

1. **Start with portfolio construction, not returns.** How many investments, what check size, how much in reserves â€” this determines everything else.
2. **Model failure explicitly.** Not all investments succeed. Model 50-70% total loss rates at seed, 30-40% at Series A. Realism builds LP trust.
3. **Sensitivity tables are mandatory.** Show how TVPI and IRR change with +/- 20% on key assumptions (graduation rates, exit multiples). One table tells LPs you're rigorous.
4. **Separate LP and GP economics.** LPs care about net returns after fees and carry. GPs care about management company sustainability + carry. Model both.
5. **Use industry-standard metrics.** Always include TVPI, DPI, IRR. LPs compare across funds using these. Non-standard metrics raise red flags.
