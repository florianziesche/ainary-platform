## 2. Major Debates & Contradictions

### Debate 1: Has AGI Arrived?

| Voice | Position | Timeline |
|-------|----------|----------|
| Altman | "Basically built" (then walked back) | Now-ish |
| Amodei | Prefers "powerful AI"—coming soon | End of 2026 |
| Sequoia | "AGI is here" via agents | Now |
| Hassabis | "Nowhere near"—needs breakthroughs | 5-10 years |
| LeCun | LLMs will never get there | Different approach needed |
| Musk | End of 2026 | Also building "singularity" (Moltbook) |

**The contradiction:** People building the same technology disagree wildly on what it is. This is either:
- A. Genuine scientific uncertainty
- B. Strategic positioning for fundraising/regulation
- C. Definitional games (functional vs. philosophical AGI)

**Investor implication:** Don't invest based on AGI timelines. Invest based on *what the technology can do today* and reasonable extrapolation.

### Debate 2: Will AI Destroy Jobs or Create Them?

**Doomsayer camp (Amodei):**
- 50% white-collar jobs gone in 5 years
- "Unusually painful" labor market shock
- AI's cognitive breadth means nowhere to hide

**Optimist camp (Huang, Horowitz):**
- Blue-collar demand surges (builders, electricians)
- Jevons Paradox: cheaper code = more demand for code
- "Innovation" path: same people, 10x output

**Middle ground (Jamie Dimon, Cognizant):**
- Government must retrain workers at local level
- $4.5T in unlocked productivity *if* businesses restructure properly
- Current adoption at ~20% in banking despite clear use cases

**The data:**
- ~245,000 tech layoffs (Q4 2025 - Jan 2026)
- 55,000 U.S. layoffs explicitly blamed on AI in 2025
- Yale study: No widespread displacement yet in macro data
- Deutsche Bank: "AI redundancy washing" — companies using AI as excuse for unrelated cuts

**Investor implication:** Job disruption is real but uneven. Bet on tools that enable the "innovation" path over pure "cost savings" plays—less regulatory risk, stickier revenue.

### Debate 3: Is This a Bubble?

**Bubble warnings:**
- Erik Gordon: "Planet-sized bubble that will pummel portfolios"
- Nanda report (Aug 2025): 95% of GenAI investments getting zero return
- Total spending expected: $1.6T by 2029

**Counter-arguments:**
- a16z: "AI lets you throw money at problems"—capital intensity is a feature, not a bug
- Real usage: Unlike Webvan, AI tools have paying customers today
- Infrastructure is being built that has real utility (data centers, chips)

**Investor implication:** The bubble narrative is too binary. Some AI investments will blow up. Others (infrastructure, vertical SaaS with AI) will compound. The skill is distinguishing.
