## 2. Universal Rules (What ALL Top Creators Do)

### The First Line Is Everything
Every single top creator obsesses over the hook. LinkedIn truncates after ~140 characters. The first 2 lines determine if anyone reads the rest.

**The anatomy of a winning first line:**
1. Creates a curiosity gap ("I made $5M and almost lost it all.")
2. OR delivers a surprising fact ("Only 1% of LinkedIn users post regularly")
3. OR challenges conventional wisdom ("Most career advice is terrible")
4. OR promises specific value ("This 3-minute framework will change how you sell")

### The "See More" Click Is the First Conversion
Think of every LinkedIn post as having TWO pieces:
- **Above the fold** (visible): Hook + 1-2 lines to create intrigue
- **Below the fold** (after "see more"): The actual value

If you don't earn the click, nothing below matters.

### Single-Sentence Paragraphs
Every top creator uses them. Not just for style ‚Äî for mobile readability. 80%+ of LinkedIn consumption is mobile. Wall-of-text = death.

### The Dwell Time Signal
LinkedIn's 2026 algorithm (360Brew AI system) heavily weights:
- **Dwell time** ‚Äî how long someone pauses on your post
- **"See more" clicks** ‚Äî signal of curiosity
- **Saves** ‚Äî strongest signal of value
- **Thoughtful comments (5+ words)** ‚Äî worth 2x a like
- **Shares with thoughts** ‚Äî highest endorsement

**The hierarchy:** Shares with context > Comments > Saves > Likes

### Engagement Scoring: The Golden Hour
Your post's fate is decided in the first 60 minutes:
1. LinkedIn shows to 2-5% of your network
2. If engagement is strong ‚Üí algorithmic amplification
3. If engagement is weak ‚Üí post dies

**Implication:** Post when your audience is online, engage immediately with early comments, have a "pod" of supportive connections ready.

### Content Stays On-Platform
Posts with external links get 40-60% LESS reach. LinkedIn wants to keep users on platform.

**What top creators do instead:**
- Put the full insight in the post itself
- If linking, put link in FIRST COMMENT, not the post body
- Create carousel/image that contains the value natively

### Authenticity > Polish
LinkedIn's algorithm now detects AI-generated content (AI hooks are 16x more common, reducing uniqueness). Posts that read as "obviously human" get priority.

**What this means:**
- Imperfections are fine
- Personal voice matters more than ever
- Specific details (names, dates, amounts) signal authenticity
- First-person experience beats generic advice

### The Spiky Point of View
Justin Welsh's concept: Every successful creator has a strong, specific, sometimes controversial perspective on their topic. Not "AI is important" ‚Äî but "AI will make 80% of marketing agencies obsolete in 3 years."

### Consistency Compounds
Every creator posts 3-7x per week. No exceptions. The algorithm rewards consistency, and audiences forget you if you disappear.

### Visual Content Dominates (2026 Update)
LinkedIn in 2026 has shifted from text-first to visual-first:
- **Multi-image posts:** 6.60% engagement rate (highest)
- **Carousels:** 5.85% engagement
- **Video:** 5.60% engagement
- **Text-only:** Declining but still effective for established voices
- **Polls:** Algorithm now penalizes them

### Post Length Sweet Spot
- **1,000-1,300 characters** consistently outperforms shorter posts (more dwell time)
- But formatting matters more than length ‚Äî a well-formatted 1,500-word post beats a poorly formatted 500-word post

## 3. Anti-Patterns (What Kills Engagement)

### ‚ùå Engagement Bait
"Comment YES if you agree!" / "Like if you think AI is the future!" ‚Äî LinkedIn's algorithm now ACTIVELY SUPPRESSES this. Instant reach penalty.

### ‚ùå External Links in Post Body
40-60% reach reduction. Always put links in first comment.

### ‚ùå Generic AI-Generated Content
LinkedIn's 360Brew AI engine detects templated, generic AI output. If your post reads like ChatGPT wrote it without editing, expect suppression.

### ‚ùå Wall of Text
No line breaks = no mobile readability = scroll-past-death. Every paragraph should be 1-2 sentences max.

### ‚ùå Starting with "I wanted to share..."
Dead hook. The moment you start with a soft, vague opener, you've lost 90% of readers.

### ‚ùå Posting About Yourself Without Universal Relevance
"I'm excited to announce I got promoted!" gets likes from friends. "Here's what I learned in 3 years that got me promoted ‚Äî and how you can use it" gets virality.

### ‚ùå Multiple Hashtags
Hashtags are nearly irrelevant in 2026. LinkedIn's semantic AI categorizes content without them. 0-3 max if you use them at all.

### ‚ùå Over-Posting (Posting Every Few Hours)
The algorithm penalizes this. Stick to 1 post per day maximum. 3-5 per week is optimal.

### ‚ùå Posting Without Engaging
If you post and ghost, the algorithm notices. Top creators spend 15-30 minutes engaging with comments after posting.

### ‚ùå Company Page vs. Personal Profile
Company pages get 5% of user feeds. Personal profiles get 65%. Always post from personal.

### ‚ùå Being Boring in the First 60 Minutes
If your post doesn't get engagement in the golden hour, it's algorithmically dead. Don't post at 2 AM when nobody's online.
