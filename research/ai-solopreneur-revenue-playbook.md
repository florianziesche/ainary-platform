## 3. Common Patterns (What ALL Successful AI Solopreneurs Do)

### Pattern 1: Build in Public
Every single case study shares their journey publicly. Pieter Levels, Marc Lou, Danny Postma, Eric Smith ‚Äî all share revenue numbers, lessons, and failures on Twitter/X. This isn't vanity ‚Äî it's **distribution**. Building in public IS the marketing.

### Pattern 2: Audience Before Product (or Simultaneously)
- Ole Lehmann: built 100K followers FIRST, then launched course ‚Üí $300K
- Ben Tossell: grew newsletter to 100K, then monetized
- Liam Ottley: YouTube channel ‚Üí community ‚Üí course ‚Üí agency clients
- **Exception: Pieter Levels and Danny Postma built products first, then audience grew from products**

**Florian's play:** Content + product simultaneously. Don't wait for audience to launch consulting. But build the audience anyway because it compounds.

### Pattern 3: Solve Money Problems, Not Tech Problems
Every successful product either:
- **Makes the customer money** (AutoShorts ‚Üí YouTube revenue)
- **Saves the customer money** (RAG consulting ‚Üí reduced search time)
- **Saves the customer time** (HeadshotPro ‚Üí no photographer needed)

The failures? They solved **tech problems** ("cool AI demo") rather than **business problems**.

### Pattern 4: Ship Speed > Code Quality
- Marc Lou: "I ship a product a week"
- Pieter Levels: 70+ launches before finding winners
- Tibo: "Ship first, think later"
- Danny Postma: HeadshotPro built in 30 days
- Maor Shlomo: Base44 from 0 to profitable in 3 months

**No one talked about clean architecture, test coverage, or technical debt.** They talked about **getting to revenue**.

### Pattern 5: One Channel, Mastered
- Danny Postma: SEO (programmatic pages)
- Eric Smith: Paid ads (Facebook/Meta)
- Ole Lehmann: Twitter/X
- Marc Lou: YouTube + Product Hunt
- Ben Tossell: Newsletter/Email

Nobody tries to be everywhere. They pick ONE acquisition channel and go all-in.

### Pattern 6: Pricing Courage
- RAG consultants went from $5K ‚Üí $100K per project
- AI consultant rates: $300-500/hour for senior experts ($895/hour for top-tier in 2024)
- Marc Lou: simple boilerplate at $199 that "any developer could build" ‚Üí $1M+
- Liam Ottley: from $5K chatbots ‚Üí $250K enterprise deals

**Every successful solopreneur undercharged at first, then dramatically raised prices ‚Äî and business improved.**

### Pattern 7: Portfolio Diversification (Eventually)
The biggest earners don't rely on one product:
- Pieter Levels: 6+ products
- Marc Lou: 10+ products
- Danny Postma: Multiple AI products (Postcrafts portfolio)
- David Park: 3 products each at $1M+ ARR

Start with ONE winner, then replicate the model.

## 4. Anti-Patterns (What Fails)

### Anti-Pattern 1: Building "AI Wrappers" Without Distribution
90% of AI startups fail. The #1 reason: **no distribution, no moat.** If your product is just "ChatGPT but for X" and you don't have a unique data advantage, workflow integration, or audience ‚Äî you're dead. 24 post-ChatGPT AI startups analyzed showed 43% failed because they built products nobody wanted.

### Anti-Pattern 2: The Margins Trap
Traditional SaaS: 80-90% gross margins. AI wrappers using API calls: 50-60% margins. If you're reselling OpenAI tokens with a UI, your economics are terrible. Winners either:
- Self-host models (Pieter Levels)
- Create value beyond the AI call (workflow, integration, data)
- Charge for outcomes, not tokens

### Anti-Pattern 3: Building for Builders Instead of Buyers
"I built an AI tool for developers" ‚Üí market of people who could build it themselves. Unless you're Marc Lou (selling time savings, not technology), building tools for technical people is harder because they always think they could DIY it.

### Anti-Pattern 4: Perfectionism Before Revenue
Spending 6+ months building before launching. Every successful case study launched in days to weeks:
- HeadshotPro: 30 days
- AutoShorts: 40 days
- Base44: 3 months to profitable
- SiteGPT: side project that took off

### Anti-Pattern 5: Selling Technology Instead of Outcomes
"We use RAG with hybrid retrieval and HNSW indexing" ‚Üí nobody cares.
"We cut your document search time by 80%, saving $200K/year" ‚Üí shut up and take my money.

### Anti-Pattern 6: Hourly Billing for AI Consulting
Hourly billing caps your income. The best AI consultants sell:
- **Project-based:** $30K-$100K per engagement
- **Value-based:** percentage of cost savings
- **Retainers:** $5K-$10K/month for ongoing advisory
- **Licensing:** build once, license to many enterprises

### Anti-Pattern 7: Pivoting Too Fast
"If it doesn't work in 2 weeks, move on." Wrong. Jenni AI took 46 months to reach $1M ARR. Pieter Levels had 70 failed projects. The trick is to **validate fast but persist on the right idea.**

### Anti-Pattern 8: Ignoring the German/European Market
Most case studies are US/English-centric. The German Mittelstand market is massively underserved for AI consulting. While everyone fights for Silicon Valley clients, German manufacturing companies with ‚Ç¨50M+ revenue are desperate for AI implementation help and have budget. **This is Florian's unfair advantage.**
