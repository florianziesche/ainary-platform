## S1: Klarna AI Agent

**Source ID:** S1  
**Title:** Klarna says its AI agent is doing the work of 853 employees  
**Authors / Organization:** Customer Experience Dive  
**Publisher / Venue:** Customer Experience Dive (Industry News)  
**Year / Date:** November 20, 2025  
**URL:** https://www.customerexperiencedive.com/news/klarna-says-ai-agent-work-853-employees/805987/  
**Source Type:** News  

**Core Claims:**
- Klarna's AI customer service agent can do the work of 853 full-time agents (up from 700 earlier in 2025)
- The AI agent has saved the company $60 million
- Klarna was labeled "the poster child for bad AI deployment" by Forrester analyst Kate Leggett
- The company "overpivoted on their AI strategy" in 2024, pausing hiring and laying off workers
- Klarna turned back to human customer service representatives in May 2024
- Customers complained the AI provided generic answers and couldn't handle nuanced questions

**Empirical Evidence:**
- AI agent equivalent to 853 full-time employees (Nov 2025)
- $60 million in cost savings (claimed)
- AI agent handled two-thirds of all customer inquiries
- Improved response times by 82%
- Decreased repeat issues by 25% since launch
- Customer service and operations cost: $50 million in Q3 2025 (up from $42 million a year ago)
- 114 million active users (up 32% from previous year)
- Q3 2025 revenue: $903 million (up 26% year over year)
- Company net promoter score: 73

**Limitations:**
- No independent verification of the $60M savings claim
- Does not detail what "scaled back" operations actually involved
- Does not specify timeline of the "reversal" or trust issues
- Customer complaints are reported through analyst, not direct data
- No breakdown of which tasks AI handles vs. humans

**Evidence Strength:** Mixed  
*Reasoning:* Financial figures are from company reports, but "reversal" narrative is primarily from analyst commentary. The $60M savings and cost increases suggest complexity in the actual business impact.

**Publisher / Venue:** BBC Travel (News)

**Core Claims:**
- Air Canada's chatbot gave incorrect information about bereavement fares in 2022
- Passenger Jake Moffatt was told he could book full-fare and apply for bereavement discount after the fact
- Air Canada argued the chatbot was "a separate legal entity responsible for its own actions"
- British Columbia Civil Resolution Tribunal ruled Air Canada liable for chatbot's misinformation

**Empirical Evidence:**
- Incident occurred: 2022
- Tribunal ruling date: February 14, 2024 (decision published)

- Ruling: Air Canada held liable for chatbot information

**Limitations:**
- Does not specify how many customers were affected
- No data on frequency of chatbot errors
- Does not describe the specific "hallucination" about bereavement fares beyond passenger claim
- No information on whether Air Canada changed chatbot systems post-ruling
- Single case study, not systemic analysis

**Evidence Strength:** Strong  
*Reasoning:* Official tribunal decision with specific ruling, damages, and legal precedent. However, limited to single incident.

**Title:** AI Agents in 2025: Why 95% of Corporate Projects Fail

**Publisher / Venue:** Directual Blog (No-Code Platform Vendor)  
**Year / Date:** November 8, 2025  
**URL:** https://www.directual.com/blog/ai-agents-in-2025-why-95-of-corporate-projects-fail  
**Source Type:** Blog / Industry Analysis  

**Core Claims:**
- 95% of organizations see no measurable return from GenAI projects
- Only 5% of integrated pilots generate millions in profit
- Gap termed "GenAI Divide" between successful and failed implementations
- 80%+ of organizations experimenting with ChatGPT/Copilot
- 40% claim deployment has started
- Only 20% reached pilot stage for custom solutions
- Just 5% made it to production
- Successful implementations show average ROI of 171% (192% in US)

**Empirical Evidence:**
- Failure rate: 95% of projects show no ROI
- Success rate: 5% reach production with measurable profit
- Experimentation rate: 80%+ use ChatGPT/Copilot
- Pilot rate: 20% reach pilot stage
- Production rate: 5% reach production
- Average ROI for successful projects: 171% (192% in US)
- Conversion rate increase: 4-7x for successful projects
- Operational cost reduction: up to 80%
- AI agent market size: $5.32B (2025) → projected $42.7B (2030), CAGR 41.5%
- 79% of organizations use AI agents
- 90% plan to expand usage
- Vendor-built tools: 67% reach production vs. 33% for internal builds

**Limitations:**
- Primary source cited is "MIT's Project NANDA report" (link provided but not independently verified in this extraction)
- Article is from no-code platform vendor (potential bias)
- "95% failure" claim lacks methodology details in article
- Statistics mix GenAI broadly with AI agents specifically
- No breakdown by industry, company size, or use case
- ZoomInfo source mentioned in search results not cited in this article

**Evidence Strength:** Mixed  
*Reasoning:* Specific statistics provided with MIT attribution, but lack of direct source verification and vendor conflict of interest. Multiple corroborating data points but methodology unclear.
