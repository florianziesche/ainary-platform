# R23: How Should an AI Agent / LLM Project Website Look?

**Status:** IN PROGRESS
**Date:** 2026-02-28
**BLUF:** The best AI/LLM product websites in 2026 follow the "Linear Look" — dark backgrounds, monochrome + 1 accent, blurry glows, thin lines, product screenshots over stock photos, and data-as-design. But execution separates elite from generic.

---

## 5 Research Topics

### Topic 1: The "Linear Look" — Why Dark Mode Became the AI Standard
**Hypothesis:** Dark mode isn't aesthetic preference — it signals technical credibility, reduces cognitive load on data-dense interfaces, and creates the visual language of "serious tool."
**Sources:** Frontend Horse "The Linear Look", LogRocket "Linear Design" (2025), Linear Brand Guidelines
**Key Findings:**
- 20+ SaaS companies copied Linear's aesthetic: Vercel, Supabase, Raycast, Clerk, Resend, Railway
- Core ingredients: Dark bg, colorful blurry glows, bento grids, product screenshots (no stock photos), super-thin lines, grid patterns, glassmorphism, gradient headings, sans-serif fonts
- **Why it works for AI:** Data-dense interfaces need dark backgrounds to reduce eye strain. Glows/gradients signal "alive, processing." Monochrome = confidence, not decoration.
- **Danger:** If everyone does it, it becomes wallpaper. Flow (wisprflow.ai) proved cream + serif can also signal premium AI.
- **For Ainary:** We're already in this camp (dark, Inter, gold accent, gradient blobs). Good. But we lack: bento grids, product screenshots, thin-line graphics, glassmorphism.

### Topic 2: Trust Signals — How AI Products Overcome the "Hype Skeptic" Problem
**Hypothesis:** AI buyers are increasingly skeptical of claims. The websites that convert best use specific trust patterns: open methodology, verifiable results, transparent limitations.
**Sources:** ebaqdesign.com AI Startup Examples (2026), Color Psychology research, inkbotdesign Startup Branding (2026)
**Key Findings:**
- **Show, don't claim:** Best AI sites show the product working (Composio: live code, Sahara: interactive 3D). Worst ones make claims without proof.
- **Specificity = trust:** "300+ sources across 10 cities" beats "AI-powered insights." Numbers with context beat adjectives.
- **EIJA/Transparency systems = differentiator:** Most AI sites have zero methodology transparency. Our EIJA system is genuinely unusual. But it needs to be PROMINENT, not buried.
- **Open source / open methodology signal:** Palantir publishes Blueprint. We publish AgentTrust. This builds structural trust.
- **For Ainary:** Our EIJA system, source counts, and confidence scores are rare in the market. Push these HARDER. Make them hero elements, not footnotes.

### Topic 3: Product Demo as Hero — Screenshots Beat Descriptions
**Hypothesis:** The single highest-converting element on AI product pages is a live or animated product demo above the fold, not text descriptions.
**Sources:** Frontend Horse, ebaqdesign patterns, Linear/Vercel/Supabase homepage analysis
**Key Findings:**
- Every elite AI site (Linear, Vercel, Supabase, Composio) shows the product IN ACTION above the fold
- Screenshots > mockups > illustrations > text descriptions (conversion hierarchy)
- Best pattern: "Product screenshot with annotated callouts" — shows what it does + why it matters
- Interactive demos (Sahara 3D element, Composio tabbed code) increase time-on-page 3-5×
- **Monitor mockup pattern** (our Kommunalwahl article) is dated (2020 skeuomorphism) — flat product card with glow is 2026
- **For Ainary:** Our dashboard IS the product. We should screenshot the actual Friedberg/Nürnberg dashboards and make them the hero. Interactive embed > static screenshot.

### Topic 4: Visual Identity — One Motif, Everywhere
**Hypothesis:** The best AI brands pick ONE visual motif and repeat it obsessively. This creates recognition without a logo.
**Sources:** ebaqdesign (Sahara logo repetition, Ventriloc pattern), Linear (mark used everywhere), inkbotdesign branding guide
**Key Findings:**
- **Sahara AI:** Repeated logo motif across entire site = instant recognition
- **Ventriloc:** Pattern from logo extended into UI charts and diagrams
- **Linear:** The mark appears in loading states, favicons, hover effects, blog headers
- **Palantir:** The hexagon appears everywhere — even in Blueprint component icons
- **60-30-10 applied to motif:** 60% clean space, 30% typography, 10% motif appearances
- **For Ainary:** Our "binary star" / gold dot is underutilized. It appears in the nav and footer but not in: blog article headers, data visualizations, loading states, chart elements, section dividers. The gold dot should be our "mark" — visible but not overwhelming.

### Topic 5: Conversion Architecture — The AI Funnel That Actually Works
**Hypothesis:** AI product websites need a different funnel than traditional SaaS because buyers need EDUCATION before they need a CTA.
**Sources:** ebaqdesign, LogRocket, Monday.com AI funnel analysis, aliapopups conversion funnel guide
**Key Findings:**
- **Traditional SaaS funnel:** Hero → Features → Pricing → CTA
- **AI product funnel:** Hero (show, don't tell) → Problem (why existing tools fail) → Evidence (data, methodology) → Demo (product in action) → Social proof → CTA
- **Key difference:** AI buyers need to trust the METHODOLOGY before they trust the product. Evidence comes BEFORE the sell.
- **Best converting CTAs for AI:** "See it in action" > "Request demo" > "Get started" > "Buy now"
- **Content as top-of-funnel:** Blog articles (our Memory article, Kommunalwahl article) are the AWARENESS stage. They need clear paths to the product, not just newsletter signups.
- **For Ainary:** Our blog articles ARE the funnel top. But the path from article → dashboard → contact is weak. Each article needs a clear "See this analysis live" link to the actual product. The dashboard IS the demo.

## Cross-Topic Patterns

1. **Data IS the design.** Don't decorate data with design. Make data the visual element. Our dashboards, charts, scores — these ARE the design.
2. **One accent, everywhere.** Gold dot, gold glow, gold accent. Not gold AND blue AND green for decoration.
3. **Show the product immediately.** Above the fold. Not after 3 paragraphs of text.
4. **Trust through transparency.** EIJA, source counts, methodology — these are competitive advantages, not footnotes.
5. **The blog IS the funnel.** Each article is a conversion path to the product.
