## Executive Summary

The CNC cost calculation and quoting software market is experiencing significant pain points with existing solutions. Survey data shows **85-90% dissatisfaction rates** with current tools, primarily due to inaccuracy, time consumption, and poor usability. The market is ripe for disruption with modern, accurate, and fast solutions.

**Key Findings:**
- Most shops still use **spreadsheets (43%)** or **"eyeball estimates" (32%)** for quoting
- Average quote-to-book ratio: **51%** (top shops: 70%) - indicating massive inefficiency
- Small CNC shops spend **2-6 hours per complex quote** manually
- ERP systems cost **â‚¬2,400-9,600/year per user** but are overkill for small shops
- German market: **~33,000 metal manufacturing SMEs**, **~5,000 CNC shops** nationwide
- DACH region represents significant untapped potential with strong manufacturing base

**Website:** digifabster.com

**Features:**

**Pricing:**

   - Inconsistent formulas across team
   - Prone to errors
   - Time-consuming (2-6 hours per complex quote)
   - No integration with CAD/CAM

2. **Eyeball Guestimate: 32%**
   - "Experienced machinist gut feeling"
   - Based on past similar parts

   - Not scalable (knowledge locked in one person's head)

3. **CAM Machining Time: 15%**
   - Accurate but **way too slow** (4-8 hours to CAM entire job)
   - Only viable for won jobs, not quotes

4. **ERP Systems: 8%**

   - Expensive, complex, underutilized

   - QuoteCam, MES, custom solutions

### 4.2 Specific Pain Points

**Time:**
- Manual calculation: **2-6 hours per complex quote**
- Top performers: **<2 minutes per drawing** (with good software)
- Average shop quotes **2 jobs for every 1 won** (51% conversion)
- Top shops: **70% quote-to-book ratio** (quoting smarter, not more)

**Accuracy:**
- Manual methods: **Â±25-50% error rate**
- Leads to:
  - Losing bids (overpriced)
  - Margin erosion (underpriced)
  - Customer distrust (inconsistent pricing)

**Inconsistency:**
- Different estimators = different quotes for same part
- No standardized process
- Knowledge loss when key person leaves

**Manual Work:**
- Tracking labor on paper/Excel
- Manual inventory counts
- Digging through spreadsheets for job details
- "Time-consuming manual tasks add up fast and easily lead to mistakes" (Global Shop Solutions)
