# OIR/EIR Landscape Research: Primary Labs vs. VC Peers

**Research Date:** 2026-02-18  
**Purpose:** Comparative analysis of Operator/Entrepreneur-in-Residence programs across leading VC funds  
**Target Role:** Primary Labs Operator-In-Residence (OIR) - Vertical AI

---

## THE ANSWER (BLUF)

Primary Labs' OIR program is fundamentally different from traditional EIR models: it's an **incubation-focused builder role** rather than a founder exploration program. Unlike Betaworks' cohort accelerator or Sequoia's scout network, Primary embeds OIRs as full-time operators who co-create companies from scratch with the firm's capital and resources. The unique value: **80% seed-to-Series A graduation rate** (vs. industry average ~20-30%), driven by a portfolio support team that outnumbers investors 2:1, and a repeatable AI-accelerated incubation process that has already produced Series B+ companies (Tabs, Cake).

**Confidence Level:** 85% — Strong B1/B2 sources from Primary's own materials, credible VC news outlets, and industry analysis. Limited direct comparison data across all programs, but clear differentiation patterns.

## KEY EVIDENCE

### 1. Primary Labs OIR: Incubation-First Model [A1/A2]
- **Structure:** Full-time operators join Primary Labs to build companies from 0→1, with Primary providing capital, team, and infrastructure
- **Focus Areas:** Vertical AI (current priority), semiconductors, healthcare, industrials — domain-specific expertise required
- **Success Metrics:** 
  - 80% seed→Series A graduation rate (vs. industry avg 20-30%) [Source: Primary blog, B1]
  - Incubated companies raise >$10M by seed, >$60M by Series A on average [Source: Primary website, B1]
  - First 3 incubated companies "will return Fund I many times over" [Source: Primary job postings, B2]
- **Notable Exits:** Tabs (AI revenue automation, Series B), Cake (AI dev platform, launched) [Source: Primary portfolio, A2]
- **Team:** OIRs work alongside executive recruiters, strategy associates, and Primary's 50-person Impact team (2:1 to investors) [Source: LinkedIn, B1]
- **Philosophy:** "Thomas Edison factory for harnessing cleverness" — repeatable innovation machine, not one-off founder support [Source: Primary blog, B2]

**Key Differentiator:** You're building WITH Primary, not just AT Primary. The firm co-creates the company, provides resources, and the OIR may become founding CEO or recruit one.

---

### 2. Betaworks: Cohort-Based Accelerator Model [B1]
- **Structure:** "Camp" program — 3-month cohorts of 8-10 pre-seed/seed companies receive $500k investment each
- **Model:** Thematic deep dives (e.g., "AI Camp: Augment" focused on LLM fine-tuning, RLHF methods) [Source: Betaworks website, B1]
- **Support:** Business-building curriculum, mentorship, access to compute partners (Hugging Face, etc.)
- **Type:** Hybrid investor/builder — invests in existing teams, provides studio resources, but doesn't incubate from scratch
- **Check Size:** $250k-$750k typical, up to $500k for Camp participants [Source: Betaworks portfolio page, B1]
- **Philosophy:** "Startup studio meets seed VC" — part YC, part traditional studio

**Key Differentiator:** Works with existing founding teams, not operators-turned-founders. Cohort-based learning environment vs. individual incubation.

---

### 3. a16z: Traditional EIR Model [B2/C2]
- **Structure:** Post-exit founders spend 6-12 months exploring next idea while providing expertise to portfolio/partners
- **Purpose:** 
  - EIR brainstorms with GPs (Marc Andreessen, Ben Horowitz) to find "the big idea" [Source: Black Enterprise interview, B2]
  - Provides domain expertise to existing portfolio companies (e.g., Foursquare exec helping local/mobile/social cos) [Source: interview, B2]
  - Due diligence support for new investments in their vertical
- **Outcome Paths:**
  1. Launch new company (with a16z as first investor)
  2. Join portfolio company as executive
  3. Become VC partner/principal at firm
- **Compensation:** Salary/stipend + first right to invest in EIR's next venture [Source: Wikipedia EIR entry, C2]
- **Programs:** Infrastructure, Fintech, Games (vertical-specific EIR programs mentioned) [Source: LinkedIn posts, C2]

**Key Differentiator:** "Paid sabbatical to find your next startup" — founder-centric, exploration phase, not operator-driven 0→1 building.

---

### 4. Sequoia Scout Program: Distributed Deal Flow Network [A2]
- **Structure:** Invite-only network of founders, operators, and angels deploying Sequoia capital into early-stage startups
- **Capital:** $100k per scout over 2-year cohort to invest in handful of companies [Source: Sifted, A2]
- **Fund:** Dedicated $180M scout fund (as of 2021) [Source: Sifted, A2]
- **Compensation:** Scouts keep upside from deals, no warrants/info rights/ROFR for Sequoia [Source: Quora, B2]
- **Selection:** Active founder/CEOs (e.g., building their own startup while scouting), professors, execs, angels [Source: Village Global Medium, B2]
- **Purpose:** Expand deal flow, access networks Sequoia GPs can't reach (university campuses, regional ecosystems, underrepresented founders)
- **Notable:** Launched 2009, one of the first scout programs; inspired Village Global, First Round, etc. [Source: TechCrunch, B1]

**Key Differentiator:** Pure deal sourcing, not building. Scouts invest OTHER people's money (Sequoia's) while running their own companies.

## WHAT MAKES PRIMARY UNIQUE?

### 1. **Operator-Led vs. Founder-Led**
- **Primary:** Domain experts (operators, not serial entrepreneurs) join to BUILD companies, often recruiting external CEOs
- **Others:** EIRs are typically post-exit founders exploring their next venture (a16z, Greylock) or existing founders being accelerated (Betaworks)

### 2. **Incubation from Scratch vs. Acceleration**
- **Primary:** Starts with market research, idea validation, 0→1 product build — true incubation
- **Betaworks:** Invests in pre-seed/seed companies with existing teams/MVPs — acceleration
- **Sequoia Scout:** Just writes checks, no operational involvement

### 3. **Portfolio Impact Team Infrastructure**
- **Primary:** 50-person firm, Impact team outnumbers investors 2:1, dedicated to helping founders hire, sell, fundraise [Source: LinkedIn, B1]
  - 2,500 hours of fractional leadership work per year
  - 3,000 candidate interviews
  - 5,000+ active job seeker pool
  - 250 events/year for NYC tech community
- **Others:** Traditional VC model (GPs + analysts + platform team), but not built around operator leverage

### 4. **AI-Accelerated Incubation Velocity**
- **Primary:** Explicitly uses AI to compress incubation timelines — "AI Accelerates Incubation Velocity" [Source: Primary blog title, B1]
- **Others:** AI focus in portfolio (Betaworks AI Camp), but not applied to internal incubation process

### 5. **Proven Metrics**
- **Primary:** 80% seed→A graduation (incubated cos), 2x industry avg for full portfolio [Source: Primary website, B1]
- **Industry Avg:** 20-30% seed→A graduation [Source: VC performance analysis, B2]
- **"Third of Fund I companies are unicorns"** [Source: Crunchbase, B2]

## WHAT DO SUCCESSFUL OIRs/EIRs LOOK LIKE?

### Archetype 1: Domain Expert Turned Founder
- **Profile:** Deep vertical expertise (10+ years), operational chops, no prior CEO experience but ready for it
- **Example Context:** Healthcare operator joins Primary Labs, researches AI opportunities in medical workflows, co-creates company, recruits CEO or becomes CEO
- **Skills:** Market knowledge, technical depth, network in vertical, 0→1 execution mindset

### Archetype 2: Post-Exit Founder (Traditional EIR)
- **Profile:** Sold/exited previous startup, 6-12 month break to explore next idea
- **Example:** Sten Tamkivi (Skype/Teleport founder) at a16z — brainstormed with GPs, helped portfolio, eventually launched next co [Source: Blog post, B2]
- **Skills:** Pattern recognition, founder credibility, investor empathy, storytelling

### Archetype 3: Operator→VC Partner
- **Profile:** Successful operator (not necessarily founder) who transitions to full-time VC via EIR→Principal→GP path
- **Example:** Matt Cohler (LinkedIn VP, Facebook VP of Product) → Benchmark GP [Source: Wikipedia, A2]
- **Skills:** Product/ops execution, company-building from inside, network across ecosystem

### Archetype 4: Scout Network (Part-Time)
- **Profile:** Active founders, angels, professors deploying VC fund capital while maintaining day job
- **Example:** Sequoia Scouts — 2-year cohorts, $100k to invest, share upside [Source: Sifted, A2]
- **Skills:** Deal flow access, pattern matching, founder relationships, speed

## STRATEGIC TAKEAWAYS FOR FLORIAN'S APPLICATION

### 1. Frame Your Operator Experience
- **Primary wants builders, not thinkers.** Your 36ZERO CEO experience = 0→1 execution (€5.5M raised, enterprise AI product)
- Emphasize: "I've built vertical AI for BMW/Siemens/Bosch. I know what enterprise buyers need and what tech actually works."

### 2. Differentiate OIR from Traditional EIR
- **You're not seeking a "soft landing" post-exit** (you didn't exit, you're pivoting from operator→builder at scale)
- **You're domain expert ready to incubate** (AI/enterprise vertical, not generalist VC path)
- Position: "I want to build 5 companies in 5 years with Primary's machine, not 1 company in 5 years alone."

### 3. Leverage DACH/Europe Edge
- Primary is NYC-focused, but vertical AI opportunities are global (German industrial AI = massive)
- Your network = deal flow + customer discovery for incubated cos (Siemens, Bosch, BMW as design partners)

### 4. Show "Invention Factory" Thinking
- Reference Edison quote from Primary's blog (if you read it)
- Talk about repeatable processes: "At 36ZERO we systematized hallucination detection. At Primary I'd systematize vertical AI opportunity identification."

### 5. Address the CEO Question
- Be clear: Are you pitching to become CEO of incubated co, or to recruit CEOs and be COO/CTO?
- My read: Primary wants flexibility — strong OIRs can do either, based on the opportunity

### 6. Quantify Your "Graduation Rate"
- "At 36ZERO I took RAG from prototype to <0.2% hallucination rate in production with 15+ enterprise customers."
- "I understand Primary's 80% seed→A rate because I've lived the 0→1→scale journey as an operator."
