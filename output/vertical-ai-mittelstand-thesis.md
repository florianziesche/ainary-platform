## THE THESIS

German Mittelstand (3.5M SMEs, €3T revenue) has 0.5% AI adoption.

Vertical AI companies that solve both will capture a generational opportunity.

1. **Manufacturing/Maschinenbau** (370K companies) → HIGHEST PRIORITY

   - Under margin pressure (EV transition)

3. **Logistics/Supply Chain** (150K companies)

   - High competition = thin margins = AI ROI matters

### Why NOW:
- **BAFA funding** (2023-2026): 50-80% grants for AI pilots

## THE GAP

### Why Mittelstand doesn't adopt AI:

1. **Cost perception:**
   - Think AI = €100K+ projects
   - Don't know about BAFA (50-80% funding)
   - Can't justify ROI without proof

2. **Trust barrier:**
   - Don't trust US SaaS companies (data sovereignty concerns)
   - Don't trust consultants (bad experiences with "Industry 4.0" hype)
   - Only trust: local peers, industry associations, proof of work

3. **Technical debt:**
   - Legacy systems (SAP R/3, custom software from 1990s)
   - No APIs, no data infrastructure
   - "AI-ready" = 3-12 months of prep work

4. **Talent gap:**
   - No in-house AI expertise
   - Can't compete with FAANG salaries
   - Need turnkey solutions (not "bring your own data scientist")

---

## THE SOLUTION: VERTICAL AI + SERVICES MODEL

### What works (learned from 36ZERO Vision):

**1. Vertical-specific, not horizontal:**
- Don't sell "AI platform"
- Sell "Predictive Maintenance for CNC machines"
- Customer knows the ROI immediately

**2. Services-first, SaaS later:**
- Start: Funded pilot (BAFA pays 50-80%)
- Prove ROI (6-12 weeks)
- Convert to recurring (SaaS or managed service)

**3. Local presence:**
- Sachsen companies buy from Sachsen
- Baden-Württemberg companies buy from Baden-Württemberg
- Founder-to-Founder, not sales rep

**4. Network effects via industry associations:**
- VEMAS (240 Maschinenbau companies)
- Robotics Saxony (70 robotics/AI companies)
- VDA, VDMA, IHK → credibility + distribution
